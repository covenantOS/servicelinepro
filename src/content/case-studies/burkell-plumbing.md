## Challenge

Burkell Plumbing in Sausalito, California, had everything a successful plumbing company needs except an online reputation that matched the quality of their work. Their team was skilled, experienced, and consistently delivered excellent results. Customers were happy. They said so in person, on the phone, and in follow-up conversations.

None of that showed up on Google.

The Marin County plumbing market is competitive and affluent. Homeowners in Sausalito, Mill Valley, Tiburon, and surrounding communities expect top-tier service and are willing to pay for it. But they also do their homework before calling. They check Google reviews. They compare star ratings. They read what other homeowners have to say. A plumbing company with a thin review profile, no matter how skilled, looks risky compared to a competitor with 50 or 100 detailed reviews.

Burkell had strong customer relationships but no mechanism to channel that goodwill into public, searchable social proof. Their satisfied customers were a silent majority, and that silence was costing the business growth.

## Strategy

ServiceLine designed a customer review participation system that made it natural and easy for Burkell's clients to share their experience publicly. The key insight was that Burkell's customers were already willing to advocate for the company. They just needed a frictionless way to do it.

After each completed service call, customers received a prompt to share their experience. The system was designed to feel like a natural extension of the service relationship, not a marketing request. The tone was professional, the process was fast, and the timing was right: customers were prompted while the quality of the work was still top of mind.

Beyond review generation, ServiceLine implemented online visibility and reputation management that ensured the growing review volume translated into tangible search ranking improvements. The Google Business Profile was optimized with accurate service descriptions, updated photos, and consistent business information across all directories.

ServiceLine also coached the Burkell team on incorporating review requests into their natural workflow. Technicians learned to mention the review process during the wrap-up conversation, which primed customers for the automated follow-up. This two-touch approach, personal mention plus automated prompt, significantly increased the conversion rate from completed job to published review.

The strategy was not about gaming the system. It was about making the truth visible. Burkell's work spoke for itself. ServiceLine gave it a microphone.

## Results

The results validated the approach within the first year:

- **20% increase in total revenue** compared to the previous period
- **75 new Google reviews** building a dominant local review profile
- **Top plumbing choice** in the Sausalito and greater Marin County area
- **Stronger conversion rates** as prospective customers saw extensive positive feedback before calling

Seventy-five reviews in the Marin County plumbing market is a significant competitive advantage. Most local plumbers have fewer than 20. The volume alone signals to prospective customers that this is a business with a track record, not a gamble.

> "Getting clientele to participate in saying great stuff about us, really getting them involved."

## Conclusion

Burkell Plumbing had a hidden asset: hundreds of satisfied customers who had never shared their experience publicly. ServiceLine converted that hidden asset into 75 visible, searchable, trust-building Google reviews. The 20% revenue increase followed because prospective customers in an affluent market do not call the plumber with the fewest reviews. They call the one with the most proof. Burkell now has that proof, and the growth reflects it.
