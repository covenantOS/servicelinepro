## Challenge

A commercial real estate agency in Austin, TX had been operating in one of the fastest-growing markets in the country but was missing the digital wave. Their average local ranking of 11.9 placed them firmly on page two of Google's local results, well outside the map pack that captures the majority of clicks.

In commercial real estate, the stakes of invisibility are enormous. A single commercial lease or property sale can generate tens of thousands in commission. Every search for "commercial real estate agency Austin" that didn't show this firm was a potential six-figure deal walking to a competitor.

The agency had a professional website, but it wasn't optimized for local search. Their Google Business Profile was sparsely filled out. They had 6 reviews compared to the top competitors' 40 to 80. And their citation profile was thin, with listings on only a handful of directories, several containing outdated office address information from a previous location.

Austin's real estate market is fiercely competitive. Dozens of agencies target the same commercial clients, and the top-ranked firms had invested in digital marketing for years. Breaking through required a focused, aggressive approach.

## Strategy

We built a map pack optimization campaign targeting "commercial real estate agency" and related search terms across Austin and surrounding areas including Round Rock, Cedar Park, and Georgetown.

The Google Business Profile overhaul started with a complete rewrite of the business description, targeting commercial real estate keywords specific to the Austin metro. We added detailed service listings for each specialization: office space leasing, retail property sales, industrial warehouse leasing, investment property acquisitions, and tenant representation. Each listing included specific descriptions with geographic modifiers.

We uploaded professional photos of their team, office, and notable properties they had brokered. We began publishing Google Posts twice weekly, featuring new commercial listings, market updates for the Austin commercial real estate market, and closed deal announcements. These posts signaled to Google that the profile was active and relevant.

Citation building targeted both real estate industry directories and Austin-area local business directories. We built listings on 55+ platforms including LoopNet, CREXi, the Austin Board of Realtors, local chamber of commerce sites, and general business directories. Every listing carried identical business information and detailed descriptions of their commercial specializations.

The review strategy focused on recent clients who had closed transactions. Commercial real estate clients are harder to solicit for reviews than residential, but the reviews carry significant weight. We drafted personalized outreach templates that the agents customized for each client. The reviews that came back were detailed, mentioning specific property types, deal values, and the agency's market knowledge.

## Results

The ranking improvement came faster than projected:

- **Average local ranking improved from 11.9 to 4.2** within five weeks
- **Map pack visibility** for "commercial real estate agency Austin" and 18 related terms
- **Profile views increased 280%** in the first two months
- **22 new Google reviews** collected, bringing their total to 28 with a 4.8 average
- **Inquiry volume increased 45%** from Google-attributed sources
- **Stronger deal pipeline** with three new commercial lease negotiations initiated from Google leads in the first 60 days

Moving from 11.9 to 4.2 pulled this agency out of page-two obscurity and into the visible map pack. In commercial real estate, where decision-makers search Google before making initial contact, that visibility gap was costing them millions in potential deal flow.

The three new lease negotiations represented properties with combined annual lease values exceeding $2 million. Even at standard commission rates, the potential revenue from those three leads alone dwarfed the cost of the entire SEO engagement.

## Conclusion

Austin's commercial real estate market rewards visibility. This agency had the expertise, the track record, and the client relationships to compete with anyone. What they didn't have was a Google presence that reflected their actual market position. Five weeks of focused local SEO closed that gap. Page two to map pack. Invisible to undeniable. In commercial real estate, the math is simple: one deal from Google search can pay for years of SEO investment.
