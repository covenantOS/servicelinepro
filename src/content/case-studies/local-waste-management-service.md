## Challenge

A regional waste management company had been providing residential and commercial dumpster rental, junk removal, and recurring trash pickup for nearly 20 years. Their fleet was large. Their service area was wide. Their Google presence was nonexistent.

With an average local ranking of 18.0, they were buried so deep in search results that no amount of scrolling would surface them for most users. Google's local map pack shows three results. The expanded view shows roughly 20. At position 18, this company was clinging to the very bottom of even the most thorough searcher's patience.

The waste management industry in their region had consolidated around two or three major players who dominated Google search. These competitors had hundreds of reviews, fully built-out profiles, and years of SEO investment. The client was losing commercial contracts to companies that showed up first on Google, regardless of price or service quality.

## Strategy

We designed a six-week optimization sprint targeting the search phrase "local waste management service" and 25+ related terms across their multi-county service area.

The Google Business Profile required a ground-up rebuild. The existing profile had a business name, a phone number, and almost nothing else. We wrote comprehensive service descriptions for each offering: residential trash pickup, commercial dumpster rental, roll-off containers, junk hauling, and recycling services. Each description targeted specific keywords tied to the services and locations they cover. We added service area designations for every city, town, and county in their coverage zone.

Photo content was a priority. We uploaded images of their truck fleet, dumpster sizes, completed cleanout jobs, and their operations facility. Google rewards profiles that demonstrate real business activity through visual content, and waste management is an industry where showing your equipment builds immediate credibility.

Citation building focused on industry-specific directories for waste haulers and environmental services, combined with general local business directories across their service area. We built 75+ citations with identical NAP data and detailed service descriptions. We also corrected 31 existing listings that had outdated phone numbers or incomplete addresses.

The review campaign targeted both residential and commercial clients. Commercial clients, in particular, were important because their reviews carry weight with Google and with other business owners evaluating waste management providers. We collected reviews that specifically mentioned service types, response times, and pricing transparency.

## Results

Six weeks from campaign launch, the transformation was complete:

- **Average local ranking improved from 18.0 to 2.4** in six weeks
- **Top 3 map pack placement** for "local waste management service" and 22 related search terms
- **Profile views increased over 500%** within the first two months
- **Inbound call volume tripled** from Google Business Profile
- **14 new commercial contracts signed** in the first 90 days, directly attributed to improved search visibility

A ranking jump from 18.0 to 2.4 is not a gradual climb. It's a complete reversal of position. This company went from the absolute bottom of local results to sitting above nearly every competitor in their market. An average ranking of 2.4 means they appear in the top three results across almost every grid point in their service area.

The commercial contracts were the real revenue driver. Each recurring commercial waste pickup contract represents thousands in annual revenue. Fourteen new contracts in 90 days represented a significant expansion of their customer base, all from clients who found them through Google search.

## Conclusion

Twenty years of quality service meant nothing on Google when the profile was empty and the citations were broken. Six weeks of focused optimization turned this waste management company from invisible to dominant. The ranking speaks for itself: 18.0 to 2.4. But the 14 new commercial contracts tell the real story. Visibility creates opportunity, and opportunity creates revenue.
