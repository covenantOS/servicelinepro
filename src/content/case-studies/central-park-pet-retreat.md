## Challenge

Central Park Pet Retreat in Salt Lake City had a loyalty problem disguised as a marketing problem. Their clients loved the facility. Pet owners raved about the care their animals received. They promised to leave reviews. And then they did not.

Manual review collection was failing at every level. Staff would remind clients at checkout. Clients would agree enthusiastically. Then they would walk out the door, get in their car, check their phone, see 14 notifications, and forget the review entirely. The business was collecting maybe one or two reviews per month from a client base that was genuinely enthusiastic about the service.

Beyond reviews, the brand itself needed work. Central Park Pet Retreat's website and visual identity did not reflect the quality of care happening inside the facility. The site was outdated, slow to load, and did not convert visitors into booked appointments. Prospective clients who searched for pet boarding in Salt Lake City and landed on the site had little reason to choose this business over competitors with sharper digital presence.

The business also lacked a paid acquisition strategy. They were relying entirely on word of mouth and the occasional organic search result, leaving significant growth potential untapped.

## Strategy

ServiceLine approached the engagement on three fronts: brand overhaul, automated review collection, and paid customer acquisition.

The branding and website rebuild came first. A modern, fast-loading site with clear service descriptions, pricing transparency, and prominent calls to action replaced the aging original. The new site was designed to convert, with booking functionality accessible from every page and trust signals woven throughout.

For review collection, ServiceLine replaced the failing manual process with an automated email system. After each boarding stay or grooming appointment, clients received a review request via email with a direct link to the Google review form. No reminders from staff. No relying on client memory. The system handled the follow-up automatically, sending a second prompt to clients who opened the email but did not complete the review.

The shift from verbal requests to email automation changed the math entirely. Staff no longer spent time on awkward checkout conversations about reviews. Clients received the prompt at a moment when they could actually act on it, sitting at their desk or on their couch, rather than standing at a counter juggling a leash and a wallet.

ServiceLine also launched targeted PPC campaigns to capture pet owners actively searching for boarding, daycare, and grooming services in the Salt Lake City area. These campaigns drove qualified traffic to the new website, where the improved conversion flow turned clicks into bookings.

## Results

The three-pronged strategy delivered measurable results within months:

- **10% increase in total revenue** over the prior period
- **95 new Google reviews** collected through the automated system
- **Streamlined operations** with staff freed from manual review collection duties
- **Higher conversion rates** from the redesigned website and PPC traffic

The 95 reviews transformed Central Park Pet Retreat's Google presence. Their listing now displays a strong star rating backed by volume that signals trustworthiness to both Google's algorithm and prospective clients scanning search results.

> "When it's through email it's so quick and easy. I truly love ServiceLine!"

## Conclusion

Central Park Pet Retreat had enthusiastic clients who never followed through on review promises. The fix was not asking harder. It was removing the friction between good intentions and completed reviews. Automated email collection, paired with a brand overhaul and paid acquisition, turned a business with a hidden reputation into one with 95 public endorsements and 10% more revenue. The clients were always willing. They just needed a system that made it effortless.
