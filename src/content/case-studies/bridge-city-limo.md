## Challenge

Bridge City Limo operated in the Portland, Oregon, luxury transportation market, a space where trust is everything. When someone books a limo for a wedding, corporate event, or airport transfer, they are not comparison shopping on price alone. They need to believe the vehicle will show up on time, look immaculate, and be driven by a professional. That belief comes from one place: what other customers have said publicly.

Bridge City had the fleet, the drivers, and the operational discipline to deliver premium service. What they lacked was the online evidence to prove it. Their Google listing had a handful of reviews. Their Yelp profile was sparse. When prospective clients searched for limo service in Portland and scanned the results, Bridge City blended into the background behind competitors who had invested in their online presence.

The luxury transportation market punishes anonymity. A wedding planner recommending limo services will not suggest a company with five reviews when a competitor has fifty. A corporate executive booking airport transfers for visiting clients will not risk a company without a substantial track record. In this industry, your review profile is your sales team, and Bridge City's sales team was understaffed.

The business was surviving on repeat clients and a small circle of referral partners. Growth had stalled because new customer acquisition depended on an online presence that did not exist in any meaningful way.

## Strategy

ServiceLine implemented a dual-platform review generation strategy targeting both Google and Yelp, the two platforms that matter most in the Portland market for luxury transportation.

The Google strategy focused on volume and recency. After each completed trip, passengers received a review prompt directing them to Google. The system was tailored to the transportation context: the prompt arrived shortly after drop-off, when the experience was still fresh and the relief of a smooth trip was top of mind. Corporate clients, wedding parties, and individual passengers all received appropriately toned requests.

The Yelp strategy required a different approach. Yelp's recommendation algorithm filters reviews aggressively, so the focus was on generating authentic, detailed reviews from established Yelp users. ServiceLine coached Bridge City's team on identifying clients who were active on Yelp and ensuring those clients received personalized follow-up.

Beyond review generation, ServiceLine built out Bridge City's online visibility across directories, maps, and search results. Consistent business information, updated fleet photos, and optimized service descriptions ensured that the growing review profile was supported by a professional digital presence.

The trust-building strategy extended to review response management. Every Google and Yelp review received a thoughtful, professional response from the business. This demonstrated active engagement and signaled to prospective clients that Bridge City valued every customer's experience.

## Results

The transformation was measured in multiples, not percentages:

- **400% revenue increase**, quadrupling the business from its pre-ServiceLine baseline
- **29 new Google reviews** building a credible and growing review profile
- **Strong Yelp presence** with reviews that survive Yelp's recommendation filter
- **Market authority** in Portland luxury transportation search results

Quadrupling revenue is a categorical shift. Bridge City went from a small operator competing for scraps to a prominent player that wedding planners, corporate travel managers, and individual clients seek out by name.

> "We have quadrupled our business since starting with ServiceLine. People reference our reviews on Google & Yelp all the time."

## Conclusion

Bridge City Limo had the service to justify premium pricing and loyal clients. What they lacked was visibility to the thousands of Portland residents and visitors searching for luxury transportation every month. ServiceLine built that visibility through systematic review generation on the two platforms that matter most. Quadrupling revenue proves that in the trust-dependent luxury transportation market, a strong review profile is not a nice-to-have. It is the engine of growth.
