## Challenge

A personal injury attorney in Dublin had been practicing for over a decade with a solid track record of case results. But their local search ranking told a different story. Their average position sat at 8.7, which placed them in the lower half of local results and completely outside the coveted Google map pack.

In legal services, the map pack is everything. When someone searches "personal injury attorney near me" after a car accident, they're not browsing casually. They need help now. They call one of the first three attorneys they see. Position 8.7 meant this firm was losing every one of those urgent searches to competitors who ranked higher but didn't necessarily deliver better outcomes.

The firm had tried running Google Ads to compensate. Personal injury keywords in Dublin cost between $75 and $150 per click. They were spending $4,000 to $6,000 monthly on ads and seeing inconsistent results. The cost per acquired client through paid search was eating into their margins.

## Strategy

We built a three-week sprint focused exclusively on map pack optimization for high-value personal injury search terms in Dublin and the surrounding area.

The Google Business Profile was the starting point. We rewrote the business description to target "personal injury attorney Dublin," "car accident lawyer," "slip and fall attorney," and 20+ additional practice-area-specific keywords. We restructured the service listings to break out each type of personal injury case they handle: auto accidents, workplace injuries, medical malpractice, premises liability. Each service received its own detailed description with relevant keywords.

We built and corrected citations across 60+ legal and general directories. Legal-specific directories like Avvo, FindLaw, Justia, and Super Lawyers received particular attention. Each listing was built with consistent NAP data and detailed practice descriptions. We also claimed listings on local business directories specific to the Dublin metro area.

Review generation was critical in legal services, where trust is the deciding factor. The firm began a systematic outreach to former clients who had expressed satisfaction with their case outcomes. Each received a personal email from the attorney with a direct Google review link. The messaging was genuine, not templated. Attorneys who write like humans get reviews. Attorneys who send form letters don't.

We also published four location-specific practice area pages on their website, each targeting a different combination of service type and geographic modifier. These pages provided substantive information about personal injury law in their jurisdiction, not thin content stuffed with keywords.

## Results

Three weeks after launch, the ranking shift was dramatic:

- **Average local ranking improved from 8.7 to 4.0** in 21 days
- **Map pack visibility** achieved for "personal injury attorney near me" and 16 related keywords
- **Consultation requests increased 65%** in the first month following optimization
- **12 new Google reviews** collected in the first three weeks, averaging 4.9 stars
- **Google Ads spend reduced by 40%** as organic visibility replaced paid positioning

Moving from 8.7 to 4.0 put this firm squarely in map pack contention for their primary keywords. For personal injury law, where a single case can be worth $50,000 to $500,000 in fees, even one additional client per month from organic search represents a massive return on the SEO investment.

The surge in consultations came from people who had been in accidents, fallen at businesses, or been injured at work. These were high-intent searchers ready to hire an attorney. The firm reported closing three new cases in the first month that came directly from improved Google visibility.

## Conclusion

Legal SEO operates under different stakes than most industries. A single ranking improvement can translate to six figures in new case revenue. This Dublin personal injury attorney stopped paying a premium for every click and started earning visibility that compounds over time. Three weeks from launch to map pack presence. The cases that followed paid for the entire engagement many times over.
