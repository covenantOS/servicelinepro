## Challenge

Starting a medical practice from zero is one of the hardest things in healthcare. LaIMA OBGYN opened in Monterey, California, with no patient base, no reviews, no search history, and no established referral network. Every other OBGYN in the market had years or decades of accumulated trust, hundreds of patient reviews, and entrenched referral relationships with local physicians.

The Monterey healthcare market is relatively small and tight-knit. Patients choose their OBGYN carefully, and they rely heavily on the experiences of other women in their community. A new practice with zero reviews is asking prospective patients to take a leap of faith, and when it comes to something as personal as obstetric and gynecological care, most people do not take leaps. They choose the provider with the longest track record.

LaIMA needed to compress years of reputation-building into months. Every week without reviews was a week where prospective patients chose a competitor instead. The clock was ticking from the day the doors opened.

## Strategy

ServiceLine deployed an automated review generation system from day one, treating reputation-building as a launch-critical priority rather than something to address later.

Every patient who visited LaIMA received a review request after their appointment. The system was designed for the healthcare context: professional in tone, respectful of patient privacy, and compliant with the expectations patients have when interacting with a medical practice. The prompt directed patients to Google, where their feedback would have maximum impact on local search visibility.

The timing was aggressive by design. A new practice cannot afford to wait for reviews to accumulate organically over two or three years. ServiceLine's system ensured that every early patient had an opportunity to share their experience, which meant the review count grew in proportion to the patient count from the very beginning.

Alongside review generation, ServiceLine implemented patient traffic optimization that combined reputation management with targeted acquisition strategies. As the review count climbed, LaIMA's Google Business Profile gained authority. Higher authority meant better placement in local search results. Better placement meant more clicks. More clicks meant more new patients. More new patients meant more reviews.

This compounding cycle is particularly powerful for new practices because each review carries more weight when the total count is low. Going from 0 to 20 reviews transforms a listing. Going from 20 to 50 makes it competitive. Going from 50 to 80 makes it dominant.

ServiceLine also ensured that LaIMA's online presence was fully optimized to capture the traffic the reviews were generating. Accurate directory listings, a complete Google Business Profile, and consistent information across all platforms meant no leads leaked through gaps in the digital foundation.

## Results

In the first year of operation, LaIMA OBGYN achieved results that typically take a new practice three to five years:

- **80% revenue increase** within the first year of operation
- **80 new Google reviews** accumulated in just 10 months
- **Market leadership** in Monterey-area OBGYN search results
- **Sustained patient acquisition** through organic search driven by review authority

Eighty reviews in 10 months for a brand new OBGYN practice is exceptional. It gave LaIMA a review profile that rivaled practices that had been operating for a decade.

> "There's no doubt that reviews are driving the traffic. We've seen tons of business growth."

## Conclusion

LaIMA OBGYN proved that a new practice does not need years to build a reputation. It needs a system that captures patient trust from the first appointment and makes it visible to every prospective patient searching Google. Eighty reviews in 10 months and 80% revenue growth in the first year turned what should have been a slow, uncertain launch into rapid market entry. The patients were satisfied from day one. ServiceLine made sure everyone in Monterey knew it.
