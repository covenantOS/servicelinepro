## Challenge

Chicago Beautiful Smiles had a review problem, but not the kind most practices face. They actually had happy patients. The issue was getting those patients to say so online.

Their existing review system required patients to complete multiple steps after leaving the office. Click a link. Create an account on a third-party platform. Navigate to the review form. Write something. Submit. Each step lost people. By the time a patient made it through the gauntlet, they had already moved on with their day. The practice was collecting maybe two or three reviews per month from a patient base that genuinely loved the care they received.

Meanwhile, their organic search visibility had stalled. The practice sat on page two of Google for "dentist in Chicago" and most neighborhood-specific searches. They were spending heavily on paid ads to compensate, but the cost per acquisition kept climbing. Without a strong review profile backing up their listings, even patients who found them organically had little reason to choose Chicago Beautiful Smiles over the dozen other options on the page.

The team knew they were leaving money on the table. They needed a system that matched the quality of their clinical work with the visibility it deserved.

## Strategy

ServiceLine replaced the multi-step review process with a frictionless system that met patients where they already were. After each appointment, patients received a single-tap prompt to leave feedback. No account creation. No navigating away from their phone. One screen, one action, done.

This was not a minor UX tweak. It was a complete overhaul of how the practice captured patient sentiment. The old system treated reviews as an afterthought. The new system made them a natural part of the patient experience, as seamless as checking out at the front desk.

On the organic search side, ServiceLine optimized the practice's Google Business Profile and built a review acquisition cadence that kept fresh, keyword-rich patient feedback flowing into their listing every week. Google's algorithm rewards recency and volume, and the steady stream of new reviews pushed Chicago Beautiful Smiles up in both map pack and organic results.

The strategy also focused on converting that new visibility into booked appointments. Review snippets appeared in search results, giving prospective patients social proof before they even clicked through to the website.

## Results

Within the first year of working with ServiceLine, Chicago Beautiful Smiles saw results across every metric that matters:

- **17% increase in total revenue** compared to the prior year
- **26 new Google reviews** with consistently high ratings
- **1 in 5 new patients** now comes directly from organic search, not paid ads
- **Team expansion** to handle the increased patient volume

The reduced friction in the review process meant the practice stopped losing willing reviewers to a clunky system. And the organic search gains reduced their dependence on paid advertising, improving margins alongside the top-line revenue growth.

> "I love how ServiceLine's program reduces the number of steps for our patients to leave feedback."

## Conclusion

Chicago Beautiful Smiles did not need more patients to say nice things about them. They needed fewer barriers between a great appointment and a public review. ServiceLine removed those barriers, and the reviews drove organic visibility that brought in new patients without additional ad spend. A 17% revenue increase and a growing team tell the story clearly: when you make it easy for satisfied patients to speak up, the market responds.
