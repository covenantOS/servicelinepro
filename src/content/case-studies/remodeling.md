## Challenge

This multi-division construction corporation operates three distinct service lines: general construction, plumbing, and roofing. Each division serves different customer segments, targets different keywords, and competes against different local companies. Managing a unified digital strategy across three separate businesses under one corporate umbrella is exponentially harder than marketing a single-service company.

The challenge was threefold. First, each division had its own website, but none were optimized for search or conversions. They existed as digital business cards, not lead generation tools. Second, the company had no coordinated advertising strategy. Each division ran sporadic Google Ads campaigns with no shared learnings, no consistent tracking, and no way to measure actual return on ad spend. Third, the divisions were cannibalizing each other's efforts. A homeowner searching for a roofer might land on the construction division site and leave confused. Internal linking between divisions was nonexistent.

The company was leaving money on the table at every turn. Customers who hired them for plumbing never learned they also offered roofing. Roofing customers did not know about their construction services. Cross-selling, the single biggest revenue opportunity for a multi-division company, was not happening digitally.

Leadership wanted a unified approach that would generate measurable leads and revenue across all three divisions while maintaining each division's distinct brand identity and market positioning.

## Strategy

ServiceLine Pro designed and executed a comprehensive digital strategy spanning SEO, website optimization, and Google Ads across all three divisions, built on a shared analytics infrastructure that tracked performance holistically.

Each division's website was rebuilt from the ground up with conversion optimization as the primary objective. Service pages were rewritten to target specific, high-intent keywords for each trade. The plumbing site targeted "emergency plumber," "water heater replacement," and "sewer line repair" in their metro area. The roofing site targeted "roof replacement," "storm damage repair," and "commercial roofing." The construction site targeted "home addition," "commercial build-out," and "tenant improvement."

Every page followed the same conversion architecture: clear service description, social proof from verified customers, transparent process explanation, and a prominent call to action above the fold. But each site maintained its own visual identity and voice appropriate to its market.

The SEO strategy operated at scale. We built content clusters around each division's core services, publishing targeted pages that addressed specific customer questions and problems. Over time, this content strategy generated organic rankings for hundreds of keywords across all three divisions, compounding traffic month after month without additional ad spend.

Google Ads campaigns were coordinated across divisions with shared conversion tracking. This allowed the team to allocate budget dynamically: when roofing demand surged after a storm, budget shifted from construction to roofing. During spring remodeling season, construction got more spend. This flexibility prevented waste and maximized return.

The cross-selling opportunity was addressed through strategic internal linking and shared retargeting audiences. A customer who visited the plumbing site started seeing ads for roofing inspections. A roofing customer received follow-up content about home additions. Each division fed leads to the others.

Unified reporting gave leadership a single dashboard showing leads, revenue attribution, and ROI across all divisions, broken down by source, campaign, and service type. For the first time, they could see exactly where their marketing dollars were working and where they were not.

## Results

The comprehensive strategy delivered results at a scale that justified the multi-division investment:

- **$2M+ in new business** directly attributed to digital marketing efforts across all three divisions
- **300+ qualified leads** generated through the combined website and advertising strategy
- **3M+ total website visits** across all division websites during the campaign period
- **Organic search traffic grew 240%** year over year through sustained SEO and content investment
- **Cross-division lead sharing** generated an additional 15% of total leads, revenue that would have gone to competitors without the unified strategy
- **Google Ads ROAS improved 180%** through coordinated budget allocation and shared conversion data
- **Cost per lead decreased across all three divisions** as SEO-driven organic traffic reduced dependency on paid advertising

The plumbing division saw the fastest initial returns due to high search volume and strong commercial intent. The roofing division showed the highest per-lead value due to larger project sizes. The construction division generated the largest individual contracts, with several projects exceeding $200,000.

## Conclusion

This case demonstrates what happens when a multi-division company stops treating each service line as a separate marketing problem and starts treating them as a connected ecosystem. The $2M+ in new business did not come from a single brilliant campaign. It came from the compounding effect of three optimized websites, three coordinated ad strategies, and one unified measurement system working together over time. The biggest unlock was cross-selling: the customer who hires you for one service and discovers you offer three others. That is the advantage a multi-division company should have, and digital strategy is how you activate it.
