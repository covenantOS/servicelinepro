## Bing and Yahoo Ads for Home Service Businesses

Every contractor running paid search focuses on Google. That is understandable. Google owns 83% of the search market. But that singular focus creates a blind spot worth millions of dollars in missed revenue across the home service industry.

The remaining 17% of search traffic flows through Bing (roughly 9%) and Yahoo (roughly 7%). Combined, these two platforms handle billions of searches per month. More importantly for contractors, the people using Bing and Yahoo are disproportionately the exact demographic you want to reach: older homeowners with higher incomes who own properties that need work.

Through our Spectrum Reach partnership, all three platforms run under one management umbrella with unified targeting, consistent ad copy, and consolidated reporting. You do not pay extra for multi-platform deployment. It is built into every campaign we manage.

This page explains why Bing and Yahoo deserve your attention, who uses these platforms, how the advertising mechanics work, and what kind of results you should expect.

## The Search Traffic Google Does Not Own

The narrative that Google owns search is partially true. In the United States, Google's desktop search market share hovers around 79%. On mobile, it climbs to roughly 88%. But these numbers obscure a critical detail: the remaining share is enormous in absolute terms.

Bing processes over 900 million searches per day globally. In the United States alone, Bing reaches over 100 million unique users per month. Yahoo, which serves Bing-powered search results through its partnership with Microsoft, adds another layer of reach across its news, finance, and email properties.

For home service businesses, ignoring these platforms means ignoring roughly one in six potential customers who are actively searching for the services you provide. These are not casual browsers. They are homeowners typing "AC repair near me" or "emergency plumber" into their search bar. They have the same urgency and intent as Google searchers. They are ready to call.

The competitive landscape on Bing and Yahoo is dramatically different from Google. Most contractors and their agencies focus exclusively on Google, which means fewer advertisers bid on Bing and Yahoo keywords. Fewer bidders means lower auction prices. Lower auction prices means your budget reaches more people for less money.

## Who Uses Bing and Yahoo: The Homeowner Profile

The demographic data on Bing and Yahoo users reads like a target customer profile for any home service business.

### Age Distribution

Bing users are significantly older than Google users. The 35-to-54 age bracket represents the largest segment, followed closely by the 55-to-64 bracket. This is the prime homeowner demographic. These are people who own established properties with aging systems, roofs approaching replacement age, plumbing that has seen decades of use, and HVAC equipment nearing the end of its service life.

Google skews younger, with a large concentration of users in the 18-to-34 bracket. Many of these users are renters who call their landlord when something breaks, not a contractor. The age distribution on Bing naturally filters toward the audience you actually want to reach.

### Household Income

Nearly 40% of Bing users have household incomes exceeding $100,000 per year. This percentage is higher than Google's user base. Higher income means greater capacity to approve repairs, replacements, and upgrades. These homeowners are less likely to comparison-shop purely on price and more likely to value quality, reliability, and professionalism.

For contractors selling premium services, think full HVAC system replacements, whole-house electrical panel upgrades, or complete kitchen remodels, the Bing audience is predisposed to afford and approve higher-ticket work.

### Homeownership Rate

Bing users have a higher homeownership rate than the general internet population. This is partly driven by the age and income demographics, and partly by the way people encounter Bing. Microsoft Edge is the default browser on every Windows PC and Surface tablet. Many homeowners, particularly those less tech-savvy, never change their default browser or search engine. They type their search into the Edge address bar, and Bing processes it.

This behavioral pattern means Bing captures a segment of the market that actively avoids or ignores Google. These are real homeowners with real service needs who will never see your Google Ads, no matter how much you spend there.

### Professional and Corporate Users

Bing is the default search engine in Microsoft corporate environments. Millions of professionals use Bing throughout their workday because their IT department configured it as the company default. When these professionals search for home services during a lunch break or between meetings, they use Bing.

This segment tends to be white-collar, higher-income, and time-constrained. They value convenience and speed over price. They want to find a reputable contractor, call, schedule, and move on. Your Bing ad catches them in this exact moment.

## The Microsoft Advertising Platform

Bing Ads operates through the Microsoft Advertising platform (formerly Bing Ads). If you have run Google Ads, the interface and concepts will feel familiar, but there are meaningful differences worth understanding.

### Campaign Import From Google

Microsoft Advertising offers a direct import tool that pulls your Google Ads campaigns into Bing with one click. Campaign structures, ad groups, keywords, ad copy, extensions, and bid strategies transfer automatically. This is how most agencies set up Bing campaigns, and it is a mistake when done without adjustment.

We import Google campaigns as a starting point, then modify them for the Bing audience. Bid adjustments account for Bing's lower competition. Ad copy gets tweaked to match Bing's slightly different character limits and ad formats. Audience targeting layers are adjusted based on Bing's demographic tools. A carbon copy of your Google campaign on Bing will perform acceptably. A Bing-optimized campaign will perform dramatically better.

### Audience Targeting on Microsoft

Microsoft Advertising offers audience targeting tools that leverage LinkedIn data, which Google cannot access. Through Microsoft's ownership of LinkedIn, you can target users by industry, job function, and company size. For home service businesses, this is useful for targeting professionals in specific income brackets and homeowners in corporate decision-making roles.

Microsoft also offers in-market audiences (people actively researching home services), custom audiences, remarketing lists, and similar audience targeting. These tools let you layer intent-based targeting on top of keyword targeting, reaching people who are both searching your keywords and exhibiting behaviors that indicate they are ready to hire.

### Ad Extensions on Bing

Bing supports a full suite of ad extensions similar to Google:

**Call extensions** display your phone number directly in the ad. On mobile, users tap to call without visiting your website.

**Location extensions** show your business address and a map pin. This is particularly valuable for home service businesses that want to communicate local presence.

**Sitelink extensions** add links below your ad to specific pages on your site: "Emergency Service," "Pricing," "Reviews," "Service Areas."

**Callout extensions** add short text snippets highlighting features: "Licensed & Insured," "Same-Day Service," "Free Estimates."

**Review extensions** display third-party review scores and snippets directly in your ad.

We configure every available extension on Bing campaigns because extensions increase your ad's visual footprint on the results page, improve click-through rates, and provide additional information that helps qualified leads self-identify.

## Why Bing CPCs Are Lower (And What That Means for Your Budget)

The cost-per-click gap between Google and Bing is one of the most compelling reasons to advertise on both platforms. Across our home service client base, Bing CPCs run 20% to 40% lower than Google for equivalent keywords.

Here is what that looks like in practice:

**HVAC Repair:** Google CPC averages $28. Bing CPC averages $18. On a $3,000 monthly budget allocated to Bing, you get approximately 167 clicks instead of the 107 clicks that budget would buy on Google.

**Plumbing Services:** Google CPC averages $35. Bing CPC averages $23. The same budget generates 56% more clicks on Bing.

**Roofing:** Google CPC averages $52. Bing CPC averages $34. High-ticket roofing keywords are expensive everywhere, but Bing offers meaningful savings at scale.

**Electrical:** Google CPC averages $22. Bing CPC averages $14. Electrical keywords already have relatively low CPCs on Google, and Bing drops them further.

The reason for lower CPCs is simple: fewer advertisers compete on Bing. Google's ad auction is crowded with contractors, national lead generation companies like Angi and HomeAdvisor, and aggressive local competitors. Bing's auction has fewer participants, which keeps prices down.

Lower CPCs do not mean lower quality leads. The conversion rates on Bing are comparable to Google, and in some trades, they are higher. A click from a high-income homeowner on Bing converts at the same or better rate as a click from a comparable searcher on Google. You get more of those clicks for the same budget.

## Yahoo: Native Ads and the News Audience

Yahoo occupies a unique position in the search and display advertising landscape. While Yahoo search results are powered by Bing (meaning your Bing ads automatically appear on Yahoo search), Yahoo also offers native advertising placements across its massive content properties.

### Yahoo Search (Bing-Powered)

When you run campaigns on Microsoft Advertising, your ads appear on both Bing.com and Yahoo.com search results. You do not need to set up a separate Yahoo campaign. This integration is automatic and included in every Bing campaign we manage.

Yahoo search users tend to skew even older than Bing users, with a heavy concentration in the 45-to-65 age range. Yahoo Mail remains one of the most-used email services among older adults, and many of these users default to Yahoo for their web searches. They are deep in the homeowner demographic.

### Yahoo Native Ads

Beyond search, Yahoo offers native advertising placements across Yahoo News, Yahoo Finance, Yahoo Sports, and Yahoo Mail. Native ads appear as sponsored content within the editorial feed, matching the look and feel of surrounding articles.

For home service businesses, Yahoo native ads serve a similar purpose to Google Display ads: brand awareness and retargeting rather than direct lead capture. A homeowner reading a Yahoo News article sees your sponsored placement about seasonal HVAC maintenance or the importance of annual plumbing inspections. The ad does not demand immediate action. It builds familiarity with your brand so that when the homeowner does need service, your company comes to mind first.

Yahoo native ads reach an audience that spends significant time consuming content on Yahoo properties. These are not quick in-and-out searchers. They are users who spend 10 to 20 minutes reading news and checking email. The extended session time means your ad gets more exposure and a better chance of being noticed.

### Yahoo Mail Ads

Yahoo Mail ads appear within the user's inbox as sponsored messages or display placements on the inbox page. With over 225 million monthly active Yahoo Mail users, this placement reaches a large audience of older, established homeowners who check their email daily.

We use Yahoo Mail ads primarily for retargeting. A homeowner who visited your website or clicked one of your search ads but did not convert sees your ad in their Yahoo Mail inbox the next day. This touchpoint keeps your brand present during the consideration phase without the higher costs of search advertising.

## Multi-Platform PPC: The Numbers That Matter

Here is a simplified example of how multi-platform deployment stretches your budget.

Imagine you are a plumbing company with a $6,000 monthly ad budget. If you spend all $6,000 on Google at an average CPC of $35, you get 171 clicks.

Now split that budget: $4,200 on Google and $1,800 on Bing. At Google's $35 CPC, you get 120 clicks. At Bing's $23 CPC, you get 78 clicks. Total: 198 clicks. That is 27 more clicks for the same budget, a 16% increase in traffic.

But the math gets better. Bing's audience converts at comparable or higher rates because of the demographic profile. If both platforms convert at 10%, the Google-only approach produces 17 leads. The multi-platform approach produces 20 leads. Three additional leads per month, twelve months per year, at an average plumbing job value of $350, equals $12,600 in additional annual revenue from the same ad spend.

Scale those numbers across larger budgets, and the impact compounds. A contractor spending $15,000 per month on multi-platform PPC versus Google-only can generate 40 to 60 additional leads per year from the Bing/Yahoo allocation alone.

## Bing Webmaster Tools and Search Console

Microsoft provides Bing Webmaster Tools, a free platform for monitoring your organic search performance on Bing. While this is separate from your paid advertising, we configure it for every client because organic and paid search work together.

Bing Webmaster Tools shows which organic keywords drive traffic from Bing, how your pages rank in Bing search results, crawl errors that might affect your Bing visibility, and backlink data specific to Bing's index.

We use this data to identify organic keyword opportunities on Bing that can be reinforced with paid campaigns, and vice versa. If your website ranks on page two of Bing for "furnace installation," we can run paid ads on that keyword to capture traffic while your organic ranking improves. Once you rank on page one organically, we shift that paid budget to another keyword where organic coverage is weak.

This organic-plus-paid strategy applies across all platforms but is particularly effective on Bing, where lower organic competition means faster ranking improvements.

## Common Objections to Bing and Yahoo Advertising

We hear the same objections from contractors who have never advertised beyond Google. Here are the facts.

### "Nobody uses Bing."

Over 100 million unique users search on Bing every month in the United States. That is not nobody. It is a massive audience that your competitors are ignoring.

### "Bing users are just people who do not know how to change their default browser."

Some are. And many of those people are homeowners over 45 with properties that need work and incomes that support hiring contractors. The reason they are on Bing is less important than the fact that they are searching for your services there.

### "I do not have enough budget for multiple platforms."

Multi-platform deployment does not require additional budget. It requires smarter allocation of the budget you already have. Moving 25% to 30% of your Google budget to Bing typically generates more total leads because of lower CPCs, not fewer.

### "My Google Ads agency already handles everything."

Most agencies either ignore Bing entirely or import Google campaigns without modification. Ask your current agency how they optimize Bing campaigns specifically. Ask about platform-specific bid adjustments, Microsoft audience targeting, and Yahoo native ad deployment. If they cannot answer with specifics, they are leaving money on the table.

### "The volume is too low to matter."

Volume is lower than Google, no question. But volume is not the metric that matters. Cost per lead is what matters. If Bing delivers 25 leads per month at $30 each while Google delivers 75 leads at $50 each, Bing is generating 25% of your leads at 60% of the cost. That is significant. That matters.
