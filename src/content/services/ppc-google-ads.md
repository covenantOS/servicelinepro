## Understanding Google's Ad Ecosystem: Search Ads vs. LSA vs. Display

Google offers three distinct advertising products. Each serves a different purpose in your lead generation strategy. Running all three creates a layered presence that captures homeowners at every stage of their search.

### Google Search Ads

Search Ads are the text-based advertisements that appear at the top and bottom of Google's search results. When a homeowner searches "plumber near me" or "emergency AC repair," your Search Ad appears with a headline, description, phone number, and link to your landing page.

You bid on specific keywords. You write the ad copy. You control which landing page the visitor sees. You pay only when someone clicks your ad. This level of control makes Search Ads the foundation of every campaign we build.

Search Ads work on an auction system. Every time someone searches a keyword you are bidding on, Google runs an instantaneous auction. Your ad position depends on two factors: your bid amount and your Quality Score. A high Quality Score means you can outrank competitors while paying less per click. We will cover Quality Score optimization in detail later on this page.

The power of Search Ads for home service businesses lies in intent. Someone searching "furnace repair" needs a furnace repaired. They are not browsing. They are not researching for a future project. They need help, and they need it now. Capturing that intent at the exact moment it surfaces is what makes Google Ads the highest-ROI advertising channel for contractors.

### Local Services Ads (LSA)

Local Services Ads occupy the very top of Google's search results, above even standard Search Ads. They display your business name, star rating, service area, hours, and a Google Guaranteed or Google Screened badge.

The pricing model differs from Search Ads. Instead of paying per click, you pay per lead. Google defines a lead as a phone call or message from a potential customer. If the lead is spam or irrelevant, you can dispute it and receive a credit.

LSAs require a background check and verification process through Google. Once approved, you receive the Google Guaranteed badge, which carries meaningful trust value. Homeowners see the badge and know that Google has verified your business, your insurance, and your licensing.

We recommend running LSAs alongside Search Ads for maximum coverage. LSAs capture the top position with trust signals. Search Ads capture the next positions with detailed ad copy and custom landing pages. Together, they give you two chances to win every search.

One important distinction: LSAs offer less control than Search Ads. You cannot write custom ad copy or direct traffic to specific landing pages. Google pulls your business information from your profile and matches you to searches based on your service categories and location. This simplicity is an advantage for smaller operations but a limitation for businesses that want precise control over their messaging.

### Google Display Ads

Display Ads appear as visual banners across Google's network of over 2 million websites and apps. Unlike Search Ads, Display Ads do not target search intent. They target audiences based on demographics, interests, browsing behavior, and retargeting lists.

For home service businesses, Display Ads play a supporting role. They are not the primary lead driver. Their strength lies in retargeting, which means showing ads to people who visited your website but did not convert. A homeowner who searched "roof replacement cost," visited your landing page, but did not call will see your Display Ads on news sites, weather apps, and YouTube for the following 30 days. This keeps your brand visible during their decision-making process.

Display Ads cost significantly less per impression than Search Ads. CPMs (cost per thousand impressions) typically run $2 to $10 for home service businesses. The conversion rate is lower because the audience is not actively searching, but the branding value and retargeting effectiveness make Display a worthwhile supplement to your Search campaigns.

## Keyword Match Types: Controlling Who Sees Your Ads

Google Ads uses match types to determine how closely a user's search query must align with your keyword before your ad is eligible to appear. Understanding match types is critical for home service campaigns because the wrong match type burns through budget on irrelevant clicks.

### Broad Match

Broad match shows your ad for searches that are related to your keyword, even if the search does not contain your exact terms. If your keyword is "AC repair," broad match might show your ad for "air conditioning troubleshooting tips," "how to fix my air conditioner myself," or "HVAC training programs."

For home service businesses, unmodified broad match is dangerous. The search queries it captures are too wide, and a large percentage of clicks will come from people who have no intention of hiring a contractor. We use broad match sparingly and only in combination with aggressive negative keyword lists and audience targeting layers.

### Phrase Match

Phrase match shows your ad for searches that include the meaning of your keyword. If your keyword is "AC repair," phrase match will trigger for "AC repair near me," "affordable AC repair," and "emergency AC repair service." It will not trigger for "AC installation" or "how to repair an AC yourself" in most cases.

Phrase match is the workhorse of our home service campaigns. It provides enough reach to capture variations of high-intent searches while filtering out most irrelevant traffic. We build the majority of our ad groups around phrase match keywords.

### Exact Match

Exact match shows your ad only for searches that have the same meaning as your keyword. If your keyword is [AC repair], your ad appears for "AC repair," "air conditioning repair," and "repair AC." It will not appear for "AC repair cost" or "AC repair reviews."

Exact match gives you the tightest control and typically delivers the highest conversion rates. We use exact match for your highest-value keywords, the terms that consistently produce booked jobs. The trade-off is lower volume. Exact match captures fewer impressions than phrase match, so we run both in parallel.

### How We Structure Match Types for Contractors

Every ad group we build contains a mix of phrase match and exact match keywords. Phrase match casts the net. Exact match captures the highest-intent searches at maximum efficiency. Broad match enters the picture only for discovery campaigns designed to find new keyword opportunities, and always with tight negative keyword protection.

This layered approach ensures coverage without waste. You reach homeowners searching for your services in dozens of different ways, but you do not pay for clicks from people looking for DIY tutorials, job openings, or wholesale equipment.

## Negative Keyword Strategy: Stopping the Bleed

Negative keywords are the most underrated element of Google Ads management. They tell Google which searches should not trigger your ads. Without a robust negative keyword list, you will hemorrhage budget on clicks that will never convert.

Home service businesses face a specific set of negative keyword challenges. Here are the categories we block on every campaign:

**Employment searches:** "jobs," "hiring," "salary," "career," "apprenticeship," "training program." People searching for plumbing jobs are not looking to hire a plumber.

**DIY searches:** "how to," "DIY," "tutorial," "step by step," "fix it yourself," "parts." These searchers want to do the work themselves. They are not your customer.

**Wholesale and equipment searches:** "wholesale," "bulk," "supplier," "distributor," "for sale." A search for "HVAC units for sale" is someone shopping for equipment, not service.

**Educational searches:** "certification," "license requirements," "school," "degree." These are trade students, not homeowners.

**Cost research without intent:** "average cost," "how much does," "price range." We block some of these selectively. A search for "AC repair cost" has moderate intent and may convert. A search for "average cost to become a plumber" has zero intent.

**Competitor brand names:** Unless you are deliberately running competitor targeting campaigns, we add competitor business names as negatives to prevent wasted spend.

Our initial negative keyword lists for home service campaigns contain 200 to 500 terms. We update these weekly using Search Terms Reports, which show the actual queries people type before clicking your ad. Every week reveals new irrelevant queries to block. This ongoing maintenance is what separates a profitable campaign from one that leaks money.

## Call-Only Campaigns: Built for the Phone

Home service businesses live and die by the phone. Between 70% and 80% of leads in the trades come through phone calls, not form submissions. Google offers a campaign type designed specifically for this reality: call-only campaigns.

A call-only ad does not send the user to a landing page. When they tap the ad on their phone, it dials your number directly. The ad displays your phone number, a headline, a description, and your business name. One tap, and the phone rings.

Call-only campaigns work exceptionally well for emergency services. When someone searches "emergency plumber" on their phone, they do not want to browse a website. They want to talk to a human who can dispatch a technician. A call-only ad eliminates every step between the search and the conversation.

We run call-only campaigns alongside standard search campaigns. Standard campaigns drive traffic to landing pages where visitors can call, fill out a form, or learn more about your services. Call-only campaigns target high-intent, urgent keywords where the fastest path to conversion is a direct call.

Bid strategies for call-only campaigns differ from standard campaigns. We set minimum call duration thresholds, typically 60 seconds, so you only pay for calls that have enough duration to represent a genuine conversation. A 5-second hang-up does not count as a conversion.

## Landing Page Conversion Optimization

Your landing page determines whether a click becomes a lead or a wasted dollar. The best keyword targeting, the sharpest ad copy, and the highest Quality Score mean nothing if the page your visitor lands on fails to convert.

We build dedicated [landing pages](/services/website-design/) for every major campaign and ad group. Each page matches the specific search intent that brought the visitor there. Here is the anatomy of a landing page that converts for home service businesses.

### Above the Fold: Instant Relevance

The top of the page answers three questions in under two seconds: Am I in the right place? Can this company help me? How do I contact them?

The headline matches the search query. If the keyword is "AC repair," the headline reads "AC Repair" or "Fast AC Repair by Licensed Technicians." Not "Welcome to Our Website." Not "Your Home Comfort Partner."

A phone number with click-to-call sits in the upper right corner and in the hero section. A short lead capture form sits beside or below the headline, asking for name, phone number, and service needed. Three fields. That is it.

### Social Proof: Why You, Not the Other Guy

Below the fold, the page displays your Google review rating, total review count, and two to three specific testimonials from customers who used the exact service being advertised. A visitor on your AC repair landing page sees AC repair testimonials, not a generic "great company" review.

Licensing badges, insurance verification, manufacturer certifications, and years-in-business callouts appear in this section. These trust signals answer the homeowner's unspoken question: "Is this company legitimate?"

### Service Details: Eliminate Objections

The middle section provides specific information about the service. What does your AC repair process include? How long does a typical repair take? Do you offer same-day service? What brands do you work on? Is there a diagnostic fee, and is it waived with repair approval?

This section heads off objections before the homeowner picks up the phone. Every unanswered question is a reason not to call.

### Mobile Optimization: Where Most Clicks Happen

Between 60% and 70% of home service searches happen on mobile devices. Every landing page we build is designed for phone screens first. Buttons are large enough to tap with a thumb. Forms use appropriate input types so phone keypads appear for phone number fields. Page load time stays under 2 seconds on 4G connections.

A landing page that looks great on desktop but frustrates mobile users will kill your conversion rate and tank your Quality Score.

## How Call Tracking Works

Call tracking is the mechanism that connects every phone call to the specific ad, keyword, and campaign that produced it. Without call tracking, you are flying blind. You know money is going out and calls are coming in, but you cannot connect the two.

Here is how our call tracking system works:

**Step 1: Number Assignment.** We provision dedicated local phone numbers for each campaign and platform. Your Google Search Ads campaign gets one number. Your Bing campaign gets another. Your LSA profile uses a third. These numbers forward to your business line, so your customers always reach your actual office.

**Step 2: Dynamic Number Insertion.** When a visitor clicks your ad and arrives on your landing page, the tracking number replaces your regular phone number on the page. This happens automatically through a JavaScript snippet. The visitor sees a local number and calls it without knowing it is a tracking number.

**Step 3: Call Data Capture.** When the call connects, our system records the caller's phone number, the call duration, the keyword that triggered the ad, the ad that was clicked, the landing page visited, the time and date, and optionally the call recording.

**Step 4: Attribution and Optimization.** This data feeds back into your campaign dashboard. You can see that "emergency plumber" generated 38 calls last month with an average duration of 4 minutes and 12 seconds. You can see that "cheap plumber" generated 15 calls with an average duration of 47 seconds, meaning most callers hung up when they heard your pricing. This data tells us exactly where to invest more budget and where to cut.

**Step 5: Recording Review.** Call recordings are available for every tracked call. You can listen to how your dispatchers and CSRs handle paid leads. Many of our clients discover that their phone handling is the bottleneck, not their ad campaigns. We have seen booking rates improve 20% to 30% after clients adjusted their phone scripts based on call recording review.

## Campaign Structure: How Everything Fits Together

A Google Ads account is organized into a hierarchy: Account > Campaigns > Ad Groups > Keywords and Ads. Understanding this structure helps you understand how we build and optimize your campaigns.

### Account Level

Your Google Ads account contains all your campaigns, billing information, and account-wide settings. You own the account. ServiceLine Pro manages it. If you ever part ways with us, the account, its data, and its history stay with you.

### Campaign Level

Each campaign represents a distinct goal or service line with its own budget, geographic targeting, scheduling, and bidding strategy. For a typical HVAC company, we build five to eight campaigns: emergency repair, standard repair, installation, maintenance, brand defense, and potentially seasonal campaigns for peak demand periods.

Campaign-level settings include daily budget caps, geographic targeting radius or zip code lists, ad scheduling (which hours and days your ads run), device bid adjustments (bidding higher on mobile for emergency searches), and audience targeting layers from Spectrum Reach data.

### Ad Group Level

Each ad group within a campaign contains a tightly themed cluster of keywords and the ads that serve them. An HVAC repair campaign might contain ad groups for "AC repair," "furnace repair," "heat pump repair," and "ductwork repair."

Tight theming matters because it allows your ad copy to match the specific search query closely. When someone searches "furnace repair" and your ad headline says "Furnace Repair," your relevance score goes up, your Quality Score improves, and your cost-per-click goes down.

### Keyword Level

Each ad group contains five to twenty keywords in phrase match and exact match variations. These are the specific searches you are bidding on.

### Ad Level

Each ad group contains three to five ad variations with different headlines, descriptions, and calls to action. Google rotates these ads and eventually favors the highest-performing variations. We test different angles: urgency ("Same-Day AC Repair"), value ("AC Repair, Free Diagnostic"), trust ("Licensed, Insured AC Repair, 4.9 Stars"), and specificity ("AC Repair for All Brands, 24/7 Service").

This structure means every dollar in your budget is precisely allocated. Emergency keywords get aggressive bids and run 24/7. Maintenance keywords get moderate bids during business hours. Brand keywords get low bids because competitors rarely challenge them. Nothing runs on autopilot without a strategy behind it.

## Quality Score Optimization

Quality Score is Google's 1-to-10 rating of how useful your ad and landing page are to someone searching your keyword. It directly affects your ad position and your cost-per-click.

A Quality Score of 8 or higher gives you a significant discount on your actual CPC. You might bid $25 but pay $18 because Google rewards your relevance. A Quality Score of 4 penalizes you, meaning you pay more than your bid to maintain position, or you lose position entirely.

Quality Score has three components:

### Expected Click-Through Rate

Google predicts how likely your ad is to be clicked based on its historical performance and relevance to the keyword. The primary driver here is keyword-to-ad alignment. When your keyword is "AC repair" and your ad headline says "Professional AC Repair," your expected CTR is high. When your keyword is "AC repair" but your ad headline says "Full-Service HVAC Company," the alignment is weaker.

We write ad copy that mirrors keyword language precisely. Every ad group gets custom headlines that reflect the exact terms in that group. No generic ads stretched across dozens of keywords.

### Ad Relevance

Google evaluates how closely your ad matches the intent behind the keyword. This goes beyond headline matching. Your description text, your display URL, and your ad extensions all contribute. An ad for "furnace repair" that mentions furnace repair in the headline, describes your furnace repair services in the description, and links to a furnace-repair-specific URL path scores high on relevance.

### Landing Page Experience

Google evaluates your landing page for relevance to the search query, mobile-friendliness, page load speed, and content quality. A landing page that matches the keyword, loads in under 2 seconds, works perfectly on mobile, and provides useful information about the service scores well.

This is where most agencies fail their clients. They send all traffic to the homepage or a generic services page. The landing page does not match the search query. Quality Score drops. Costs rise. Conversions fall. Our approach of building dedicated landing pages for every major ad group keeps landing page experience scores consistently high.
