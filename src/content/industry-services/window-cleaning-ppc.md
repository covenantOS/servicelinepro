## Why Google Ads Works for Window Cleaning

Window cleaning occupies a sweet spot in the Google Ads ecosystem. The keywords are affordable. The decision cycle is short. And the customer lifetime value, when you account for annual rebookings and service bundling, is substantial.

### Low Cost Per Click

Window cleaning keywords cost $8 to $25 per click in most markets. Compare that to plumbing at $30 to $60 per click or HVAC at $25 to $50 per click. This lower cost means your budget stretches further. A $3,000 monthly budget generates 150 to 300 clicks. At a 20% conversion rate, that is 30 to 60 leads per month.

The lower CPC also makes Google Ads viable for smaller window cleaning companies that might not have the budget to compete in more expensive industries. A $1,500 to $2,000 monthly budget can generate meaningful lead flow for a window cleaning company, whereas the same budget in plumbing or HVAC might only produce a handful of leads.

### High Conversion Rates

Window cleaning has one of the highest conversion rates in home services. Homeowners searching for a window cleaner are making a relatively simple purchase decision. They do not need three quotes. They do not need to compare material options. They need clean windows, and they need someone to come do it.

This short decision cycle means that a well-built landing page converts a high percentage of clicks into calls. While HVAC companies might see 8% to 12% conversion rates, window cleaning companies routinely achieve 15% to 25%. That higher conversion rate compounds the advantage of the lower CPC.

### Customer Lifetime Value

A first-time window cleaning customer who has a good experience becomes a recurring customer. Most residential window cleaning customers rebook two to four times per year. At an average price of $200 to $400 per visit, a single customer is worth $400 to $1,600 per year. Over five years, one customer acquired through a $15 Google Ads click could generate $2,000 to $8,000 in revenue.

When you factor in this lifetime value, the true cost per acquisition through Google Ads becomes almost negligible. A $15 click that converts into a customer worth $5,000 over five years delivers a return on investment that few marketing channels can match.

## Campaign Structure for Window Cleaning

We structure Google Ads campaigns for window cleaning companies around service types, geography, and seasonal demand patterns.

### Residential Window Cleaning Campaign

This campaign targets homeowners with keywords like "window cleaning near me," "residential window cleaning," "house window washing," "home window cleaners," and "window cleaning service [city]." Ad copy emphasizes the convenience of professional window cleaning, the visual difference of clean windows, and any bundled service options.

The landing page for residential campaigns features photos of sparkling clean residential windows, pricing information for typical home sizes, and a simple booking process. Homeowners want to know two things: how much does it cost and when can you come. Answer both quickly.

### Commercial Window Cleaning Campaign

Commercial window cleaning commands higher project values and often involves ongoing contracts. Keywords for this campaign include "commercial window cleaning," "office window cleaning," "storefront window cleaning," "building window washing," and "window cleaning service for businesses."

Ad copy for commercial campaigns emphasizes reliability, insurance coverage, capacity to handle multi-story buildings, and your ability to work around business hours. The landing page should include commercial project photos, information about your safety certifications, and details about your scheduling flexibility.

### Service Bundling Campaign

Many homeowners who search for window cleaning also need pressure washing, gutter cleaning, or general exterior maintenance. We create campaigns targeting bundled service searches like "window and gutter cleaning," "exterior home cleaning," "window cleaning and pressure washing," and "house washing service."

These bundled keywords often have lower competition than individual service terms because fewer companies optimize for them. They also produce higher-value leads because the customer is ready to book multiple services in a single visit.

### Seasonal Campaign Adjustments

We adjust bids, budgets, and ad copy seasonally to align with demand cycles. During spring cleaning season, we increase budgets by 30% to 50% and update ad copy to reference spring cleaning, new season visibility, and pollen removal. During fall, ad copy shifts to pre-holiday cleaning, entertaining guests, and closing up for winter.

During slower months, we reduce budgets but maintain visibility for year-round opportunities like commercial cleaning, interior window cleaning, and post-construction cleanup. This approach prevents you from disappearing during the off-season while concentrating the majority of your spend during high-demand periods.

## Landing Page Strategy

Window cleaning landing pages must accomplish two things quickly: build trust and make it easy to book. Homeowners searching for window cleaning do not want to read a novel. They want to see that you are professional, know your pricing, and schedule the job.

### Pricing Transparency

Window cleaning is one of the few home services where publishing pricing on your landing page improves conversion rates. Homeowners expect to see ranges like "$150 to $250 for a typical 2,000 sq ft home" or "$5 to $10 per window." Publishing pricing pre-qualifies leads, reduces tire-kicker calls, and sets expectations before the first conversation.

If your pricing varies by window type, building height, or accessibility, provide representative ranges with a note that exact pricing depends on a quick assessment. Most homeowners understand this. They want a ballpark, not a binding quote.

### Before-and-After Gallery

Window cleaning before-and-after photos are inherently compelling. The visual contrast between dirty and clean glass is dramatic and immediate. Include three to five before-and-after pairs on your landing page. These photos do more selling than any paragraph of text.

### Online Booking Option

If your scheduling system supports it, offer an online booking option directly on the landing page. Many homeowners prefer booking online to making a phone call, especially younger homeowners. Even a simple contact form that asks for address, number of windows, and preferred date provides enough information to schedule an estimate or confirm a booking.

### Reviews and Trust Signals

Include your star rating, total review count, and three to five review excerpts on the landing page. For window cleaning, reviews that mention the quality of the cleaning, the professionalism of the crew, and the ease of the booking process are most persuasive.

## Negative Keywords

Proper negative keyword management prevents your budget from bleeding away on irrelevant clicks. Common negative keywords for window cleaning campaigns include:

"Jobs" and "hiring" to exclude job seekers. "DIY" and "how to" to exclude people who want to clean their own windows. "Tint" and "tinting" to exclude window tinting searches. "Replacement" and "repair" to exclude window replacement and repair searches. "Blinds" and "curtains" to exclude window covering searches. "Washing machine" to exclude appliance searches that overlap with "window washing" terms.

We build these exclusions before launch and expand them weekly based on search term report data. In the first month of a campaign, we typically add 50 to 100 negative keywords based on actual search queries that triggered your ads.

## Geographic Targeting

We configure geographic targeting to match your actual service area with precision. For window cleaning companies, this often means targeting specific zip codes or a radius around your base of operations rather than an entire metro area.

We also adjust bids by location. Affluent neighborhoods with larger homes and more windows generate higher average ticket values. We bid more aggressively for searches from these areas and reduce bids for areas where homes are smaller and average job values are lower.

This geographic bid optimization ensures your budget is concentrated where it produces the highest-value leads. A click from a homeowner in a neighborhood of $500,000 homes is worth more than a click from someone in an area of $150,000 condos. Your bid strategy should reflect this.

## Tracking and Performance Measurement

We install comprehensive tracking on every campaign. Call tracking with dynamic number insertion records every phone call generated by your ads. Form submission tracking captures every contact request. Both data streams feed back into Google Ads for optimization.

For window cleaning companies, we track these key metrics:

**Cost per lead.** How much you pay for each phone call or form submission. Target: $15 to $40.

**Cost per booking.** How much you pay for each confirmed job. Target: $25 to $75.

**Average job value.** The revenue generated per booked job. This lets us calculate true ROI.

**Customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value.** For companies that track rebooking rates, we compare the cost of acquiring each new customer against their projected lifetime revenue.

Monthly reports break down performance by campaign, keyword, geography, and device type. You see exactly which keywords produce your most cost-effective leads and where opportunities exist to scale.
