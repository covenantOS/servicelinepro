## Keyword Strategy by Service Category

Concrete contractors need segmented campaigns because the service range is broad and each service attracts a different customer.

### Driveway Keywords

Driveways are the highest-volume residential concrete keyword category:

- "concrete driveway installation [city]"
- "driveway contractor near me"
- "driveway replacement [city]"
- "new concrete driveway cost [city]"
- "driveway repair near me"

Driveway searches indicate homeowners dealing with cracked, sunken, or stained driveways ready for replacement, or new construction homeowners selecting a contractor. Average project value: $4,000 to $12,000.

### Patio and Outdoor Living Keywords

Patio keywords capture homeowners investing in outdoor living spaces:

- "concrete patio contractor [city]"
- "patio installation near me"
- "stamped concrete patio [city]"
- "backyard patio contractor"
- "concrete patio cost [city]"

Patio projects range from $3,000 for a basic 200 square foot slab to $25,000 or more for large decorative installations with built-in seating, fire pit pads, and multi-level designs.

### Decorative Concrete Keywords

These keywords attract premium customers willing to pay for aesthetic upgrades:

- "stamped concrete contractor [city]"
- "stained concrete floors [city]"
- "exposed aggregate driveway [city]"
- "polished concrete [city]"
- "decorative concrete [city]"

Decorative concrete clicks may cost more but produce projects with 30 to 60 percent higher margins. A stamped concrete patio at $15 to $25 per square foot is far more profitable than a brushed finish at $6 to $12.

### Foundation Keywords

Foundation searches indicate urgent, high-value needs:

- "foundation repair [city]"
- "foundation contractor near me"
- "slab foundation repair [city]"
- "foundation crack repair [city]"

Foundation keywords are expensive, often $25 to $40 per click, but project values of $5,000 to $30,000 justify the cost. Foundation repair customers are motivated by structural concern and often make decisions quickly.

### Commercial Keywords

- "commercial concrete contractor [city]"
- "concrete flatwork contractor"
- "parking lot concrete [city]"
- "warehouse floor contractor"

Lower volume, but single contracts can generate $20,000 to $500,000 in revenue.

### Negative Keywords for Concrete Contractors

Essential negatives to prevent budget waste:

- "concrete" + "mix" / "bags" / "home depot" / "lowes" (material buyers)
- "how to pour concrete" / "DIY concrete" (self-doers)
- "concrete countertops" (different trade unless you offer this)
- "concrete blocks" / "cinder blocks" (masonry material searches)
- "concrete pump rental" / "concrete mixer rental" (equipment rentals)
- "concrete jobs" / "concrete laborer" (employment searches)

We maintain negative keyword lists of 300 plus terms for concrete campaigns and refine them weekly.

## Campaign Architecture

### Campaign 1: Residential Driveways and Flatwork

Targets driveway installation, replacement, sidewalk, and general flatwork keywords. Receives the largest share of residential budget due to volume.

## Ad Copy for Concrete Services

**Pricing Anchors:** "Concrete Driveways from $8/sq ft" or "Stamped Patios Starting at $14/sq ft" give homeowners the cost framework they need. Concrete is priced by the square foot, and homeowners know this. Give them the number.

**Portfolio Proof:** "500+ Driveways Poured" or "See Our 200+ Patio Projects" quantifies your experience. Concrete is a permanent installation, and homeowners want a contractor with a proven track record.

**Speed Messaging:** "Free Estimates Within 48 Hours" or "Schedule Your Pour This Month" addresses the common frustration of contractors who are slow to quote or booked out indefinitely.

**Trust Markers:** "Licensed, Bonded, and Insured" "25 Years in [City]" "Rated 4.9 Stars on Google" addresses the trust barrier for a high-dollar home improvement project.

**Material Expertise:** For decorative campaigns, showcase specific capabilities: "20+ Stamped Patterns Available" "Custom Color Matching" "Authentic Stone and Wood Textures."

## Landing Page Strategy

Concrete services demand visual proof. Landing pages must feature high-quality project photos prominently.

**Service-Specific Pages:** A click on "stamped concrete patio" should land on a page showcasing stamped patio projects with pattern options, color samples, pricing ranges, and an estimate request form. Not a general concrete services page.

**Galleries Above the Fold:** For decorative concrete pages, place a project gallery or hero image above the fold. The visual impact of a stunning stamped patio or polished floor is your strongest conversion tool.

**Process Transparency:** Walk the homeowner through what happens after they request an estimate. Site visit, measurements, material selection, base preparation, pour day logistics, curing time, and sealing. This reduces anxiety about a complex, permanent home improvement project.

**Pricing Ranges:** Concrete customers want to know approximate costs before committing to an estimate visit. Provide per-square-foot ranges for each service. This transparency builds trust and pre-qualifies leads so your estimators spend time with serious buyers.

## Seasonal Budget Strategy

**March through May:** Ramp to peak budget. Spring is booking season for concrete work. Homeowners plan projects, request estimates, and schedule pours. Capture this demand aggressively.

**June through August:** Maintain peak budget. Pour season is underway. Crews are busy, but lead generation must continue to fill the schedule through fall.

**September through November:** Reduce to 70 percent of peak in temperate markets, 50 percent in cold climates. Fall provides a secondary booking window before winter.

**December through February:** Reduce to 40 to 50 percent of peak in warm markets. In cold climates, shift budget toward interior services (polished floors, garage coatings) and foundation work. Maintain enough presence to capture spring planners.
