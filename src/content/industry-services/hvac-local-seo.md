# Local SEO for HVAC Companies

The HVAC industry runs on seasonal urgency. When the AC fails on the hottest day of summer, the homeowner does not ask friends for recommendations. They search Google. "AC repair near me." "HVAC company [city]." "Air conditioner not working." Google shows them three companies in the Map Pack, and those three companies capture the calls.

Your HVAC company needs to be in that top three for both heating and cooling searches across every city and neighborhood you serve. That requires a local SEO strategy built around the seasonal demand patterns, equipment diversity, and competitive dynamics unique to the HVAC industry.

HVAC local SEO is more complex than most home service trades because you are effectively marketing two distinct businesses under one brand. Air conditioning services and heating services have different keywords, different seasonal peaks, different customer urgency levels, and different competitive landscapes. A strategy that treats them as one monolithic category will underperform in both.

## Seasonal Keyword Strategy

HVAC search demand follows temperature. Understanding these patterns and building your content calendar around them is the foundation of effective HVAC local SEO.

### Cooling Season Keywords (May through September)

Air conditioning searches explode when temperatures rise. The first heat wave of the year triggers a massive spike in search volume as homeowners discover their AC is not working after sitting idle all winter.

**High-volume cooling keywords:**
- "AC repair near me" (peak volume June and July)
- "Air conditioning service"
- "AC not blowing cold air"
- "AC unit not working"
- "Central air repair"
- "AC refrigerant recharge"
- "Air conditioner installation cost"
- "New AC unit cost"

**High-value cooling keywords:**
- "AC replacement cost" (average job value $5,000 to $12,000)
- "Central air installation" ($4,000 to $10,000)
- "Ductless mini split installation" ($3,000 to $8,000)
- "SEER rating explained" (research-phase, leads to equipment sales)

Begin publishing cooling-focused GBP posts and blog content in March and April, before demand peaks. By the time homeowners start searching in May, your fresh content has been indexed and your GBP shows recent activity related to AC services.

### Heating Season Keywords (October through March)

Heating searches follow the mirror pattern. The first cold snap triggers furnace repair and replacement searches.

**High-volume heating keywords:**
- "Furnace repair near me"
- "Heating repair"
- "Furnace not working"
- "No heat in house"
- "Furnace making noise"
- "Heater blowing cold air"

**High-value heating keywords:**
- "Furnace replacement cost" ($3,000 to $8,000)
- "Heat pump installation" ($4,000 to $12,000)
- "Boiler replacement cost" ($5,000 to $15,000)
- "Radiant floor heating installation" ($6,000 to $15,000)

Publish heating-focused content starting in August and September. Fall is also the peak time for furnace maintenance searches, which feed your maintenance agreement pipeline.

### Shoulder Season Keywords (March-April, September-October)

Shoulder seasons present opportunities for maintenance and equipment replacement marketing.

**Maintenance keywords:**
- "AC tune-up"
- "HVAC maintenance near me"
- "Furnace tune-up"
- "Air conditioning maintenance"
- "HVAC inspection"

**Equipment research keywords:**
- "Best AC units 2026"
- "Trane vs Carrier"
- "Heat pump vs furnace"
- "HVAC system cost"
- "Ductless mini split reviews"

Homeowners researching equipment during shoulder seasons are planning purchases for the upcoming season. These leads take longer to close but involve the highest ticket values.

## Google Business Profile Optimization for HVAC

Your GBP is the control center of your HVAC local SEO. Every element needs optimization for both heating and cooling services.

### Category Strategy

**Primary category:** HVAC Contractor

**Additional categories:**
- Air Conditioning Contractor
- Heating Contractor
- Air Conditioning Repair Service
- Furnace Repair Service
- Duct Cleaning Service
- Heat Pump Installer

Each category creates additional keyword associations. "Furnace Repair Service" as a category means your listing can appear for "furnace repair near me" even if that exact phrase does not appear in your GBP description. Most HVAC companies leave multiple categories unused, giving you an easy competitive advantage by filling all available slots.

### Service List Optimization

List every HVAC service individually with detailed descriptions:

Instead of "AC Repair," write: "Air conditioning repair for all brands including Trane, Carrier, Lennox, Rheem, and Goodman. We diagnose and repair refrigerant leaks, compressor failures, capacitor issues, thermostat malfunctions, blower motor problems, and frozen evaporator coils. Same-day AC repair available."

This level of detail matches your profile against long-tail searches like "Trane AC compressor repair" and "frozen evaporator coil repair near me."

### Seasonal GBP Post Strategy

Your GBP posting schedule should rotate with the seasons:

**Spring (March to May):** AC tune-up specials, new AC installation promotions, indoor air quality content, before-and-after photos of AC installations
**Summer (June to August):** Emergency AC repair availability, energy efficiency tips, new system installations, customer testimonials about fast AC repair
**Fall (September to November):** Furnace tune-up reminders, heating system inspection offers, heat pump content, maintenance agreement promotions
**Winter (December to February):** Emergency heating repair availability, furnace replacement content, indoor air quality during closed-house season

Post at least twice per week. Each post should include a photo (preferably geotagged from a recent job), a service-relevant description, and a call-to-action button.

### Photo Optimization

HVAC companies should upload diverse photos to their GBP:
- Completed equipment installations (indoor and outdoor units)
- Your team in branded uniforms
- Your service vehicles
- Indoor air quality equipment installations
- Before-and-after ductwork photos
- Thermostat installations
- Maintenance work in progress

Geotag every photo with the job site location before uploading. Over time, a library of geotagged photos across your service area creates strong geographic signals that help you rank in neighborhoods beyond your immediate office location.

## Maintenance Agreement Pages and SEO

Maintenance agreements are the most valuable customer acquisition tool in HVAC. A homeowner who signs a maintenance agreement becomes a recurring revenue customer who is also pre-sold on future equipment replacement. Optimizing your website to rank for maintenance-related keywords feeds this pipeline directly.

### Dedicated Maintenance Page Content

Create a comprehensive maintenance agreement page with:

**Plan tier breakdown.** Most HVAC companies offer two to three tiers. A basic tier covering one annual tune-up, a standard tier covering two seasonal tune-ups (heating and cooling), and a premium tier adding priority scheduling, discounted repairs, and extended warranties. Present these tiers with clear pricing and a comparison table.

**What each tune-up includes.** List the specific inspection and maintenance tasks performed during each visit. "Our 22-point AC tune-up includes refrigerant level check, condenser coil cleaning, evaporator coil inspection, blower motor lubrication, electrical connection tightening, thermostat calibration, and safety control verification." Specificity demonstrates value and differentiates you from competitors who just say "annual tune-up."

**ROI justification.** Explain the financial benefit. "Regular maintenance extends equipment lifespan by 5 to 7 years. A $200 annual maintenance plan saves $3,000 to $5,000 in premature replacement costs." Include energy savings data. "A properly maintained AC unit operates 15 to 25 percent more efficiently than a neglected one, reducing your energy bill by $200 to $400 per year."

**Online signup functionality.** Allow homeowners to purchase maintenance agreements directly from the page. An online payment option reduces friction and converts visitors who might not want to call.

### Maintenance Keyword Targeting

Build content around these high-intent maintenance keywords:
- "HVAC maintenance plan near me"
- "AC tune-up cost"
- "Furnace tune-up near me"
- "HVAC annual service plan"
- "Air conditioning maintenance"
- "Spring AC tune-up"
- "Fall furnace tune-up"

Each keyword variation could warrant its own landing page if search volume justifies it. "AC tune-up" and "furnace tune-up" should definitely have separate pages since they serve different seasonal intents.

## Local Area Pages for HVAC Companies

Build dedicated pages for every city, neighborhood, and significant geographic subdivision in your service territory.

### HVAC-Specific Area Content

Generic area pages that swap city names are worthless. Each page must contain content specific to that area's HVAC needs:

**Climate considerations.** Different areas within your service territory may have different HVAC needs. Coastal areas need corrosion-resistant outdoor units. Areas with extreme temperatures need higher-capacity systems. Urban areas with older homes often have ductwork that needs modification for modern equipment.

**Housing stock details.** Reference the typical home age, construction type, and existing HVAC systems. "Homes in [neighborhood] were built primarily between 1975 and 1985. Most have original forced-air systems with 80% AFUE furnaces and 10 SEER air conditioners. These systems are well past their expected lifespan and replacing them with modern 95%+ AFUE furnaces and 16+ SEER AC units reduces energy costs by 30 to 40 percent."

**Local utility information.** Mention the local utility company and any rebate programs for energy-efficient HVAC equipment. "Homeowners in [city] served by [utility company] qualify for up to $1,500 in rebates for installing ENERGY STAR certified heat pumps."

**Common problems.** Address HVAC issues specific to that area. Neighborhoods with mature trees may have condenser coils clogged with cottonwood or pollen. Areas near the coast deal with salt air corrosion. Older neighborhoods may have asbestos-wrapped ductwork that requires special handling.

## Review Strategy for HVAC Companies

HVAC companies have two distinct review generation opportunities that correspond to their seasonal peaks.

### Service Call Reviews

Emergency repair calls generate the highest emotional gratitude and the best reviews. A homeowner whose AC was restored on a 100-degree day or whose furnace was fixed during a cold snap writes detailed, emotional reviews that resonate with future customers.

Send review requests within 2 hours of completing emergency repairs. The emotional peak fades quickly. A review request sent the next day converts at half the rate of one sent the same afternoon.

### Maintenance Visit Reviews

Maintenance visits are lower-emotion interactions, but they happen in high volume and provide consistent review flow during shoulder seasons when emergency calls slow down. Train maintenance technicians to mention the upcoming review request before leaving. "We're going to send you a quick link in a couple hours to leave us feedback. It really helps our business."

### Review Response Strategy

Respond to every review mentioning the specific service performed. "Thank you, Mike. Glad we could get your Trane XR15 back up and running before the weekend heat wave. The capacitor replacement should keep it running strong." This response contains brand names, service details, and implied timeliness that future customers notice.

## Competing with HVAC Franchises and National Brands

HVAC is heavily franchised. One Hour Heating & Air, Aire Serv, Service Experts, and similar national brands have significant online presence. Local HVAC companies compete by:

**Dominating hyper-local content.** Franchises cannot create neighborhood-specific pages for every location they serve. Build deep local content that references specific neighborhoods, local utility programs, and area-specific HVAC considerations.

**Emphasizing brand-neutral expertise.** Franchises often push proprietary equipment brands or exclusive manufacturer partnerships. Position yourself as brand-agnostic: "We install and service all major HVAC brands including Trane, Carrier, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, Daikin, and Mitsubishi." Homeowners who want the best equipment for their home, not the brand the franchise profits most from, respond to this messaging.

**Building authentic review volume.** Franchise locations share review profiles with national brands, which can dilute local authenticity. A local HVAC company with 500 genuine reviews from customers in the specific service area carries more trust than a franchise location riding on the national brand's reputation.

## Technical SEO for HVAC Websites

Your website architecture should create clear pathways for both search engines and users to find content for any HVAC service in any location you serve.

### Content Hierarchy

Homepage > Service Category (Heating, Cooling, Indoor Air Quality) > Individual Services (Furnace Repair, AC Installation, Duct Cleaning) > Area-Specific Pages

This hierarchy creates a logical sitemap that search engines crawl efficiently and users navigate intuitively.

### Equipment and Brand Pages

Create pages for the major HVAC brands you install and service. "Trane HVAC Installation and Repair in [City]" targets homeowners searching for brand-specific services. These pages describe the brand's product lineup, your certification or training with that brand, and why you recommend (or do not recommend) specific models.

### Internal Linking

Link heating services to relevant area pages. Link cooling services to relevant area pages. Link equipment brand pages to both service and area pages. Create a mesh of internal links that helps Google understand the relationship between your services, your locations, and your expertise.

## Measuring HVAC Local SEO Performance

Track these HVAC-specific metrics monthly:

**Seasonal ranking comparison.** Compare your Map Pack positions for AC keywords in summer to heating keywords in winter. Identify which season needs more optimization work.

**Maintenance agreement signups from organic search.** Track how many maintenance customers enter through your website versus other channels. This metric demonstrates the long-term revenue value of local SEO beyond one-time service calls.

**Revenue per keyword category.** Attribute closed revenue to the keyword categories that generated the leads. Emergency repairs, equipment replacements, and maintenance agreements each have different values and different costs to acquire.
