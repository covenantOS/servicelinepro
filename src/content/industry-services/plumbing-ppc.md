# Google Ads for Plumbing Companies

A homeowner with water pouring through their ceiling does not scroll through organic search results. They click the first result that looks like it can solve their problem right now. Google Ads puts your plumbing company in that position: the first thing a desperate homeowner sees when they need a plumber.

The economics of Google Ads for plumbing companies are compelling when managed properly. Plumbing CPCs average $25 to $53 per click depending on the keyword and market. That sounds expensive until you calculate the return. A single emergency call generates $300 to $800 in revenue. A water heater replacement generates $2,000 to $5,000. A sewer line repair generates $3,000 to $10,000. Converting just one in every five to ten clicks into a paying customer produces returns of 5x to 20x on your ad spend.

The problem is that most plumbing companies run Google Ads poorly. They target broad keywords, send traffic to their homepage, ignore the search terms report, and wonder why they are spending $5,000 a month with nothing to show for it. This guide covers how to build and manage Google Ads campaigns that generate consistent, measurable plumbing leads.

## Campaign Architecture for Plumbing Companies

Plumbing covers too many distinct services to run a single campaign. Each service category has different keyword intent, competition levels, and customer behavior. Separate campaigns allow you to allocate budget, write ad copy, and build landing pages optimized for each category.

### Campaign 1: Emergency Plumbing

Emergency keywords carry the highest intent and the highest CPCs in plumbing PPC.

**Keywords:**
- "Emergency plumber near me"
- "24 hour plumber"
- "Plumber open now"
- "Burst pipe repair"
- "Sewer backup emergency"
- "Gas leak plumber"
- "Flooding in house plumber"

**Bid strategy:** Maximize conversions with target CPA. Emergency plumbing leads are worth $300 to $800 per call minimum, so a CPA target of $80 to $150 is profitable. Bid aggressively on these terms because every competitor is also bidding aggressively, and falling off the first page means zero visibility.

**Ad copy:** Lead with availability and speed. "Emergency Plumber. Available Right Now. Fast Response. Call (555) 123-4567." Homeowners with active emergencies do not read long ad descriptions. They scan for confirmation that you are available and then call.

**Schedule:** Run 24/7 if you answer calls around the clock. If you have an answering service for after-hours, still run 24/7 but adjust bids down 20% during hours when your office is not directly answering. If you only take calls during business hours, pause the campaign outside those hours. Paying $45 for a click that goes to voicemail is burning money.

**Landing page:** Phone number dominant. Above the fold: "Emergency Plumber. We Answer 24/7. Call Now." Short form as backup for homeowners who prefer not to call. Three trust signals (licensed, insured, years in business). No navigation menu. No distractions from the conversion action.

### Campaign 2: Water Heater Services

Water heaters represent the highest-ticket recurring opportunity in residential plumbing. Every water heater in every home will eventually need repair or replacement, and the homeowner's first instinct is to search Google.

**Keywords:**
- "Water heater repair near me"
- "Water heater replacement cost"
- "Tankless water heater installation"
- "No hot water"
- "Water heater leaking"
- "50 gallon water heater installation"
- "Water heater pilot light won't stay lit"

**Bid strategy:** Target CPA with a ceiling of $100 to $200. Water heater replacement jobs average $2,000 to $5,000, making a $150 cost per lead extremely profitable. Water heater repair calls average $200 to $500 but frequently convert to replacement sales when the unit is old or failing.

**Ad copy:** Address the specific problem when possible. For "no hot water" searches: "No Hot Water? We Fix & Replace Water Heaters. Same-Day Service Available." For "water heater leaking" searches: "Water Heater Leaking? Call Now Before It Gets Worse. Licensed Plumber. Same-Day Repair or Replacement."

**Landing page:** Dedicated water heater page with repair vs replacement content, brand options (Rheem, A.O. Smith, Bradford White, Navien for tankless), pricing ranges, and a prominent financing callout for replacements.

### Campaign 3: Drain Cleaning

Drain cleaning is a volume play. Lower ticket values ($150 to $400 per call) but higher search volume and lower CPCs than emergency or water heater keywords.

**Keywords:**
- "Drain cleaning near me"
- "Clogged drain"
- "Sewer line cleaning"
- "Hydro jetting"
- "Kitchen sink clogged"
- "Slow drain"
- "Main line backup"

**Bid strategy:** Maximize clicks with a maximum CPC cap. Drain cleaning CPCs are typically $10 to $25, and the service is straightforward enough that you can handle high volume. The key metric is total lead volume, not individual lead value.

**Ad copy:** Service-specific and solution-focused. "Clogged Drain? Same-Day Drain Cleaning. $99 Any Drain Special. Licensed Plumber." If you run a drain cleaning special, lead with the price in your ad copy. Drain cleaning is a commodity service where price transparency wins clicks.

### Campaign 4: Sewer and Pipe Services

High-ticket, low-volume campaigns targeting homeowners with major plumbing infrastructure problems.

**Keywords:**
- "Sewer line repair"
- "Sewer line replacement cost"
- "Trenchless sewer repair"
- "Repiping house cost"
- "Slab leak repair"
- "Camera sewer inspection"
- "Sewer line camera inspection"

**Bid strategy:** Manual CPC with high bids on top-of-page keywords. These searches are rare but each lead is worth $3,000 to $15,000. A $60 CPC with a 10% conversion rate is a $600 cost per lead for a $5,000+ job. The math works.

**Ad copy:** Expertise and technology. "Trenchless Sewer Repair. No Digging Required. Camera Inspection Included. Licensed & Insured." Homeowners facing sewer line replacement are scared of the cost and disruption. Messaging that reduces perceived risk converts well.

## Call Extensions and Call-Only Campaigns

Plumbing is a phone-first industry. Over 70% of plumbing leads from Google Ads come through phone calls, not form submissions. Your campaign structure should reflect this reality.

### Call Extensions

Add call extensions to every campaign. Call extensions display your phone number directly in the ad, allowing mobile users to call without visiting your website. Track calls from extensions separately from landing page calls to understand their contribution.

### Call-Only Campaigns

Run parallel call-only campaigns for your highest-intent keywords. Call-only ads only show on mobile devices and the entire ad is a call button. When a user taps the ad, their phone dials your number directly.

Call-only campaigns produce leads at a lower cost per lead than standard campaigns because every click is a phone call. There is no landing page friction, no form abandonment, and no bounce rate. The tradeoff is lower click volume because call-only ads only appear on mobile.

For emergency plumbing keywords, call-only campaigns consistently outperform standard search campaigns on cost per lead. A homeowner searching "burst pipe plumber" on their phone at midnight wants to make a call, not browse a website.

## Location Targeting Strategy

Plumbing companies serve defined geographic areas. Your location targeting should match your actual service territory precisely.

### Radius Targeting

Set a radius around your business location that matches your realistic service area. A plumbing company in a dense urban market might use a 15-mile radius. A company in a suburban or rural market might need a 30 to 50-mile radius. Do not target areas you cannot serve with reasonable response times.

### Location Bid Adjustments

Increase bids for zip codes closest to your office, where you can respond fastest. Decrease bids for the outer edges of your service area, where response times are longer and you may lose leads to more local competitors.

Analyze your historical data to identify which zip codes produce the most revenue per lead. A wealthy suburb with older homes and a high concentration of water heater and repiping needs might justify a 30% bid increase. A zip code that generates mostly low-ticket drain cleaning calls might warrant a 15% bid decrease.

### Neighborhood-Level Targeting

Google Ads allows targeting by city, zip code, and in some cases, neighborhood. For plumbing companies, targeting specific neighborhoods with older homes (1950s to 1970s construction with galvanized pipes and aging sewer lines) produces higher-value leads than targeting newer subdivisions.

Research the age of housing stock across your service area. Homes built before 1970 are prime candidates for repiping. Homes built before 1990 likely have original water heaters approaching or past their expected lifespan. Target these areas more aggressively for high-ticket service campaigns.

## Negative Keyword Management for Plumbing

Plumbing keywords attract enormous volumes of irrelevant traffic without proper negative keyword management.

**Immediate negative keywords to add:**
- DIY, how to, tutorial, fix myself
- Jobs, career, hiring, salary, apprentice, journeyman (job seekers)
- Supply, wholesale, parts, home depot, lowes (people buying parts)
- Free, cheap, discount (low-quality leads)
- Commercial, industrial (unless you serve commercial)
- Specific competitor names (unless running competitor campaigns)

**Problem keywords that need ongoing management:**
"Plumber" triggers searches for "plumber's crack," "Super Mario plumber," and "plumber salary." "Pipe" triggers searches for "smoking pipe," "pipe organ," and "pipeline." "Drain" triggers searches for "brain drain," "drain tile," and video game references. Review your search terms report weekly and add irrelevant terms as negative keywords.

During your first month running plumbing ads, expect to add 50 to 100 negative keywords. By month three, the list will exceed 200. This ongoing maintenance is the single most important factor in preventing wasted spend.

## Ad Scheduling and Dayparting

Plumbing demand follows predictable hourly patterns that your bid strategy should mirror.

**Early morning (6 AM to 9 AM):** High-intent searches. Homeowners wake up to discover plumbing problems overnight. No hot water for a morning shower triggers urgent searches. Increase bids 15 to 20 percent.

**Mid-morning to early afternoon (9 AM to 2 PM):** Steady volume for both emergency and scheduled services. Maintain standard bids.

**Late afternoon (2 PM to 6 PM):** Decision-making window. Homeowners who noticed issues during the day search for plumbers before evening. Maintain standard bids.

**Evening (6 PM to 10 PM):** Emergency searches increase. Homeowners discover problems after returning from work. If you offer after-hours service, maintain or increase bids. If you do not, reduce bids or pause.

**Late night (10 PM to 6 AM):** Lower volume but extremely high intent. Every search at 2 AM is a genuine emergency. If you offer 24/7 service and answer the phone, maintain bids. The competition is lowest during these hours, which means your CPCs drop significantly while lead quality remains high.

## Tracking and Attribution

Plumbing companies must track every lead from click to closed job to understand which campaigns, keywords, and ads generate revenue.

### Call Tracking

Use dynamic number insertion on your landing pages. Each visitor sees a unique phone number that ties their call to the specific keyword, ad, and campaign that brought them. This data reveals which keywords generate $5,000 water heater replacement calls versus which keywords generate $150 drain cleaning calls.

Record calls with proper disclosure. Listen to a sample weekly to evaluate lead quality and identify opportunities to improve ad targeting. If your "water heater repair" campaign generates calls asking about water softeners, your keyword targeting or ad copy needs refinement.

### Conversion Tracking

Set up conversion tracking for both phone calls and form submissions. Track call duration (calls under 60 seconds are rarely legitimate leads) and form completions separately. Use Google's offline conversion import to feed actual revenue data back into the platform, enabling smart bidding algorithms to optimize for revenue rather than just lead volume.

### Cost Per Lead by Service Category

Calculate your cost per lead for each campaign separately. Emergency plumbing leads at $100 each are profitable when average emergency tickets are $500+. Drain cleaning leads at $100 each are marginal when the average drain cleaning ticket is $200. This data drives budget allocation decisions. Shift budget from low-ROI campaigns to high-ROI campaigns continuously.

## Competitor Strategy

Research your competitors' Google Ads presence before and during your campaigns.

Search your target keywords and document which competitors appear, what their ads say, and where their ads link. Identify gaps in their messaging that you can exploit. If no competitor mentions financing, lead with financing. If no competitor promotes same-day service, make that your headline.

Monitor your auction insights report monthly. This report shows which competitors you are competing against most frequently, their impression share, and their average position relative to yours. If a new competitor enters the auction and drives up your CPCs, you may need to adjust bids or refine targeting to maintain profitability.

Consider running competitor brand name campaigns if local regulations allow it. Bidding on "Roto-Rooter" or "Mr. Rooter" in your market captures homeowners who search for franchise names but might prefer a local plumber. Your ad copy should emphasize what differentiates you: "Looking for a Local Plumber? Licensed. Insured. Family-Owned Since 2005."

## Budget Allocation for Plumbing PPC

For a plumbing company spending $3,000 per month on Google Ads:

- Emergency Plumbing Campaign: $900 (30%)
- Water Heater Campaign: $750 (25%)
- Drain Cleaning Campaign: $600 (20%)
- Sewer/Pipe Services Campaign: $450 (15%)
- Call-Only Campaign: $300 (10%)

Adjust allocations based on seasonal demand. Water heater search volume increases in winter when cold incoming water makes failing heaters more noticeable. Drain cleaning demand spikes in spring when root growth accelerates. Emergency plumbing demand is relatively consistent year-round.
