## Why Google Ads Works for Pressure Washing Companies

Pressure washing combines two characteristics that make Google Ads highly effective: affordable cost per click and high customer lifetime value. A click costs $5 to $18. A well-run campaign converts 12 to 18 percent of clicks into leads. That produces leads at $15 to $40 each. A single residential house washing at $300 to $500 returns the cost of 10 to 15 clicks. And the customer who books house washing this spring will book again next spring, creating recurring revenue that compounds the initial acquisition value.

The math gets even better with commercial accounts. A property management company that hires you for monthly building washing at $800 to $2,000 per month represents $10,000 to $24,000 in annual revenue from a single lead that may have cost $30 to acquire.

## Campaign Structure

### Campaign 1: Core Residential

Targets "pressure washing near me" and "power washing near me" with city variations. Highest budget allocation for maximum residential lead volume.

### Campaign 2: House Washing

Dedicated to house washing keywords. Ad copy emphasizes whole-house pricing, soft wash capability for delicate siding, and visual transformation results.

### Campaign 3: Driveway and Concrete

Driveway, sidewalk, and concrete cleaning keywords. Ad copy mentions oil stain removal, sealing options, and before-and-after results.

### Campaign 4: Roof Cleaning

Roof washing and soft wash keywords. Requires different ad messaging because roof cleaning involves specific technique knowledge and homeowner concerns about damage.

### Campaign 5: Commercial

Building washing, storefront, parking lot, and fleet washing keywords. Ads emphasize scheduling flexibility, volume pricing, and maintenance contracts.

## Ad Copy That Converts

**Pricing Transparency:** "House Washing from $199" "Driveway Cleaning Starting at $99" "Full Exterior Package: $499" Pressure washing customers shop on price more than most home services because they perceive the work as less complex. Clear pricing attracts clicks from customers in your target budget range.

**Visual Results:** "See the Transformation" "Before and After Results" "Your House Will Look Brand New" Pressure washing is one of the most visually satisfying services. Ad copy should promise the dramatic transformation customers are seeking.

**Speed:** "Next-Day Service Available" "Book Today, Clean Tomorrow" Pressure washing customers want their property cleaned soon, often before a specific event (party, home sale, HOA deadline).

**Method Expertise:** "Soft Wash Safe for All Siding Types" "No-Damage Guarantee" "Professional Equipment, Not Rental Machines" Address the concern that high pressure can damage surfaces.

**Reviews:** "500+ Five-Star Reviews" "Rated #1 Pressure Washing in [City]" In a market with many operators of varying quality, social proof differentiates professional companies from part-time operators.

## Recurring Revenue Through Ads

Pressure washing has natural recurring demand. The house you wash this spring will need washing again next spring. The commercial property you clean monthly will need cleaning every month for years.

Your Google Ads strategy should capture first-time customers and then convert them to annual or recurring service through follow-up marketing. The initial customer acquisition cost from ads is justified not by the first job alone but by the lifetime value of a customer who books annually for three to five years.

We help pressure washing companies build remarketing campaigns that target past visitors and customer lists with seasonal booking reminders: "Spring is here. Book your annual house washing." This remarketing loop turns one-time ad-acquired customers into recurring revenue.

## Seasonal Budget Strategy

**January through February:** Baseline budget. Begin increasing in late February as spring demand starts building.

**March through May:** Peak budget. Increase 40 to 60 percent above baseline. Spring cleaning and pre-summer preparation drive highest search volumes.

**June through August:** Maintain elevated budget. Summer demand stays strong for residential and commercial services.

**September through October:** Reduce to baseline. Moderate demand as the season winds down in temperate markets.

**November through December:** Reduce to 60 to 70 percent of baseline. Demand softens in most markets. Holiday cleaning creates some pre-gathering demand.
