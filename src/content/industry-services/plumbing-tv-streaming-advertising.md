## How Connected TV Reaches Your Customers

Connected TV encompasses every device used to stream video content on a television screen. Roku, Amazon Fire Stick, Apple TV, smart TVs, gaming consoles. The content comes from platforms like Hulu, Peacock, Paramount+, Tubi, Pluto TV, and hundreds of others that serve ads during programming.

Your plumbing company's commercial plays within this content during natural commercial breaks. The viewer experience mirrors traditional television, but the targeting capabilities are dramatically more precise.

### Targeting for Plumbing Companies

Spectrum Reach accesses household-level data on over 30 million homes across 36 states. For plumbing companies, we build targeting profiles that concentrate your budget on the households most likely to need plumbing services:

**Homeowners only.** Renters call their landlord when they have a plumbing problem. Homeowners call a plumber. We exclude renters entirely.

**Home age filters.** Homes built before 1980 often have galvanized or cast iron drain lines approaching end of life. Homes built between 1980 and 2000 may have polybutylene supply lines prone to failure. Homes with these older plumbing systems generate the most emergency and replacement work.

**Geographic precision.** Your ads run only in the zip codes you serve. A plumbing company covering a 25-mile radius does not pay to reach households 50 miles away.

**Home value targeting.** Higher-value homes generate larger average tickets. A whole-house repipe in a $400,000 home is a different project than one in a $150,000 home. Targeting by home value lets you focus on your most profitable customer segment.

**Behavioral data.** Spectrum Reach can identify households that have recently shown interest in home improvement content, home services, or real estate (which often triggers plumbing inspections and repairs).

### Reach and Frequency Planning

For plumbing companies, we design campaigns to achieve a frequency of six to eight impressions per household over a 30-day period. Research consistently shows that this level of repetition moves a brand from unknown to recognized.

A typical plumbing company campaign targeting a single metro area reaches 40,000 to 150,000 households. Over a 90-day campaign, the cumulative exposure builds substantial brand equity in your market. Homeowners begin to recognize your trucks, your logo, and your company name when they encounter it anywhere, including in search results.

## Creative Strategy for Plumbing Companies

Plumbing commercials must accomplish two objectives: communicate reliability and plant your name in the viewer's memory. You have 15 or 30 seconds. Every frame and every word must earn its place.

### The Emergency Scenario

The most effective plumbing commercials open with a relatable emergency. Water pooling on a bathroom floor. A homeowner staring at a water heater that has stopped working. A kitchen faucet that will not turn off. These scenarios trigger an emotional response because every homeowner has experienced or feared a plumbing emergency.

The opening establishes the problem. The next beat shows your team solving it. Uniformed technicians arriving in a clean, branded truck. A professional assessment of the issue. Skilled repair work captured on camera. The homeowner's relief when the problem is resolved.

This narrative arc, from problem to solution, works because it mirrors the actual customer experience. The viewer sees themselves in the homeowner's shoes and associates your company with the resolution.

### Trust Signals

Plumbing requires entering a customer's home. That demands trust. Your commercial must communicate trustworthiness through:

**Uniformed technicians.** Clean uniforms with your logo project professionalism.

**Branded vehicles.** A fleet of wrapped trucks signals an established operation, not a solo operator working out of a personal vehicle.

**Licensing and insurance.** A brief mention establishes legitimacy.

**Years in business.** "Serving [City] for 25 years" communicates permanence.

**24/7 availability.** If you offer emergency service, say so. It is a powerful differentiator.

### Memorable Branding

The viewer must remember your company name after the spot ends. This requires repetition within the commercial itself. Display your logo throughout. Mention your company name at least twice in the voiceover. End with your name, phone number, and a brief tagline if you have one.

Colors matter. If your brand colors are distinctive, use them prominently. Red trucks, blue uniforms, green logos. Visual distinction aids recall.

A jingle or audio signature, even a brief one, dramatically increases brand recall. The human brain remembers sounds and melodies more easily than visual information alone.

## Seasonal Campaign Strategy

Plumbing demand follows seasonal patterns that influence streaming TV strategy.

**Winter** drives emergency calls for frozen and burst pipes, water heater failures, and heating system issues in homes with hydronic heating. We increase frequency and shift creative to winter-specific messaging during cold months.

**Spring** brings sewer line issues as tree roots that expanded during wet months infiltrate aging drain lines. Spring is also when homeowners schedule maintenance they delayed during winter.

**Summer** generates demand for water line repairs, irrigation system connections, and bathroom remodels. Creative shifts to project-based messaging.

**Fall** is water heater season. Homeowners notice their water heater struggling as temperatures drop and cold groundwater makes the unit work harder. We push water heater messaging heading into fall.

We adjust your streaming TV campaign quarterly to align with these demand patterns, ensuring your messaging resonates with the plumbing needs homeowners are most likely experiencing at that time of year.
