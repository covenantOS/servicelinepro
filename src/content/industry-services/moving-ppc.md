# Google Ads for Moving Companies

Moving companies face a lead quality problem that Google Ads can either solve or worsen. The moving industry is plagued by tire-kickers who request quotes from five companies, price shoppers looking for the absolute cheapest option, and people who ultimately decide to rent a U-Haul. A poorly managed Google Ads campaign burns budget on all three.

A well-managed campaign filters out low-quality traffic, targets homeowners ready to hire professional movers, and generates leads that convert into booked moves at profitable margins. The key is understanding what separates a good moving lead from a bad one and building campaigns that attract the former while repelling the latter.

Moving company Google Ads also require aggressive seasonal management. Moving volume fluctuates 300 to 400 percent between winter lows and summer peaks. Your ad spend, bid strategy, and campaign structure must adapt to these cycles or you will either miss the summer surge or waste money during the winter slowdown.

## Campaign Structure for Moving Companies

### Campaign 1: Local Moving

Targets homeowners moving within your metro area. The highest volume, most consistent campaign.

**Keywords:**
- "Moving company near me"
- "Local movers [city]"
- "Movers near me"
- "Moving company [city]"
- "Apartment movers"
- "Home movers near me"
- "Full service movers"

**Bid strategy:** Target CPA of $40 to $80. Local moves average $500 to $2,000 in revenue. A $60 cost per lead with a 30% booking rate produces a $200 cost per booked move on a $1,000 average ticket.

**Ad copy:** Trust and transparency. "Licensed & Insured Local Movers. Flat-Rate Pricing. No Hidden Fees. Free Quote in 10 Minutes." Moving customers are conditioned to expect surprise charges. Messaging that addresses this fear directly outperforms generic moving ads.

**Landing page:** Quote request form with fields for move date, origin zip, destination zip, approximate home size (studio, 1-bed, 2-bed, 3-bed, 4-bed+), and phone number. Include your hourly rate or minimum charge to set expectations. Show reviews prominently.

### Campaign 2: Long-Distance Moving

Targets customers moving across states or long distances. Higher ticket values but longer decision timelines.

**Keywords:**
- "Long distance movers"
- "Interstate moving company"
- "Cross country movers"
- "Out of state moving company"
- "Moving from [city] to [city]"

**Bid strategy:** Target CPA of $80 to $150. Long-distance moves generate $3,000 to $10,000 in revenue. Higher CPAs are justified by the dramatically higher ticket values.

**Ad copy:** Credibility and logistics expertise. "Licensed Interstate Movers. DOT Certified. Dedicated Trucks (No Shared Loads). GPS Tracking. Guaranteed Delivery Window." Long-distance moving customers worry about their belongings being on a truck for days. Messaging that addresses tracking and dedicated transport builds confidence.

**Landing page:** Detailed form collecting origin city, destination city, move date (or date range), home size, and any special items (piano, hot tub, antiques). Include a brief overview of your long-distance process and timeline. Show your DOT/FMCSA number and licensing.

### Campaign 3: Specialty Moving

Targets specific moving needs that carry higher ticket values or unique requirements.

**Keywords:**
- "Piano movers near me"
- "Office movers [city]"
- "Commercial moving company"
- "Senior moving services"
- "Furniture delivery service"
- "Gun safe movers"
- "Hot tub movers"

**Bid strategy:** Maximize conversions. Specialty moves command premium pricing ($500 to $3,000 for piano moves, $2,000 to $20,000 for office moves). Let Google's algorithm optimize for conversions across the diverse keyword set.

**Ad copy:** Specialized expertise. For piano movers: "Professional Piano Movers. Padded, Insured, Experienced. Uprights, Grands, and Baby Grands." For office movers: "Commercial Moving Company. Minimize Downtime. Weekend & After-Hours Moves. IT Equipment Handling."

### Campaign 4: Call-Only

Running parallel call-only campaigns for your highest-intent local moving keywords generates leads at the lowest cost per acquisition.

**Keywords:** "Movers near me," "moving company near me," "same day movers," "last minute movers."

**Schedule:** Business hours only when your phones are staffed. Moving quote calls require someone who can collect details and provide estimates. Sending call-only traffic to voicemail wastes the entire click.

## Lead Quality Filtering

Lead quality is the defining challenge of moving company PPC. Without active filtering, 30 to 50 percent of your leads will be unqualified.

### Ad Copy Filtering

Include pricing signals in your ad copy to pre-qualify clicks:
- "Local Moves Starting at $350"
- "2-Bedroom Moves From $800"
- "Full-Service Moving. Professional Crew. Starting at $150/Hour"

Homeowners expecting to pay $100 for a full home move will not click an ad that says "Starting at $350." This self-selection reduces wasted clicks from price shoppers who would never book at your rates.

### Negative Keywords for Moving Companies

**Critical negative keywords:**
- U-Haul, Penske, Budget (truck rental searchers)
- Rental, truck rental, van rental (DIY movers)
- Free, cheap, cheapest, discount (extreme price shoppers)
- DIY, self-service, self-move
- Jobs, career, hiring, driver (job seekers)
- Boxes, supplies, tape, dolly (supply shoppers)
- Storage only (unless you offer standalone storage)
- Junk, removal, hauling (junk removal, not moving)

Moving keywords trigger an extraordinary volume of irrelevant searches. "Movers" triggers searches for "movers and shakers," movie references, and exercise content. "Moving" triggers "moving to [city]" searches from people researching a city, not hiring movers. Aggressive negative keyword management is essential.

### Landing Page Qualification

Your landing page form should include qualifying fields:
- Move date (filters out people "just researching")
- Origin and destination addresses (confirms the move is real)
- Home size (indicates revenue potential)
- Special items (piano, safe, pool table signal higher-ticket moves)

A form with these fields produces fewer submissions but dramatically higher lead quality. Every submission represents a real person with a real move who has provided enough information for you to estimate the job and prioritize follow-up.

## Seasonal Budget Management

Moving demand follows one of the most extreme seasonal patterns in home services.

### Peak Season (May through September)

Increase budget by 50 to 100 percent above your baseline. June, July, and August generate 40 to 50 percent of annual moving volume. Search volume for moving keywords triples compared to winter months.

During peak season, increase bids for top-of-page positions. Every moving company increases their budget in summer, so competition rises. Maintaining visibility requires higher bids. The increased cost per click is offset by higher conversion rates as more genuine movers enter the market.

### Shoulder Seasons (March-April, October-November)

Maintain baseline budget. Spring moving picks up in March as families prepare to move before the school year ends. Fall sees steady volume from job relocations and lease turnovers.

### Off-Season (December through February)

Reduce budget by 30 to 50 percent. Moving volume drops significantly in winter. Maintain campaigns at reduced bids to capture the smaller volume of winter movers who have less competition for their attention.

Exception: end-of-year corporate relocations can justify maintaining higher budgets through mid-December. Military PCS (permanent change of station) moves also occur year-round and represent high-value long-distance opportunities.

### Monthly Timing

Within each month, moving searches spike around the 1st and 15th (lease turnover dates) and during the last week of the month as renters prepare for next month's move-in. If your budget allows, increase daily budgets by 20 to 30 percent during these periods.

## Call Tracking for Moving Companies

Moving companies generate the majority of their revenue from phone-based quote conversations. Without call tracking, you cannot measure which campaigns produce bookable leads.

### Dynamic Call Tracking

Use unique phone numbers on each landing page tied to specific campaigns and keywords. When a prospect calls from your local moving landing page, you know they searched "movers near me" or a similar local keyword. When they call from your long-distance page, you know they searched an interstate keyword.

### Call Recording and Scoring

Record calls and score them:
- **Quoted:** Caller provided enough information for a quote
- **Booked:** Quote was given and move was booked
- **Unqualified:** Wrong service, out of area, unrealistic budget, information seeker

This scoring lets you calculate cost per booked move by campaign, not just cost per call. A campaign that produces 50 calls per month but only 10 bookable leads has a very different value than one producing 30 calls with 20 bookable leads.

### Revenue Attribution

Track each lead through your booking process. When a lead from Google Ads books a $3,000 long-distance move, that revenue should attribute back to the specific keyword and campaign that generated it. This data drives budget allocation decisions.

## Retargeting for Moving Companies

The moving decision process often spans 2 to 4 weeks from initial research to booking. Retargeting keeps your company visible throughout this period.

**Retargeting audiences:**
- Local moving page visitors who did not submit a form (7-day window)
- Long-distance page visitors who did not submit a form (14-day window)
- Quote form starters who did not complete submission (7-day window)
- Previous customers who may need storage or additional services (30-day window)

**Retargeting messaging:**
- "Still planning your move? Get your free quote today."
- "Book your summer move early. Crews fill up fast."
- "Transparent pricing. No hidden fees. Get your quote."

## Competing with Lead Aggregators

Moving companies face intense competition from lead aggregator websites like Moving.com, HireAHelper, and MovingCompanyReviews. These sites bid aggressively on moving keywords and sell the same lead to multiple moving companies.

Google Ads lets you compete directly with aggregators by appearing alongside or above them in search results. Your advantage: homeowners who click your ad get connected directly to your company instead of getting sold to five competitors simultaneously.

Highlight this in your ad copy: "Get Your Quote Directly. No Middlemen. No Spam Calls From 5 Companies. One Call, One Quote, One Company."
