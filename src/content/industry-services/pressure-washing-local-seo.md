### Seasonal Patterns

**Spring (March through May):** The primary booking season. Homeowners emerge from winter to find green algae on siding, black stains on roofs, and mildew on decks. Search volume surges 50 to 80 percent above winter baseline. Content optimized for spring searches should be published by February.

**Summer (June through August):** Peak season. Volume stays elevated. Real estate agents drive demand for pre-listing cleanings. Outdoor entertaining season pushes deck and patio cleaning searches.

**Fall (September through November):** Secondary peak in some markets as homeowners prepare for holidays and clear mildew before winter.

**Winter (December through February):** Lowest volume in cold climates. Steady in Sun Belt markets where year-round outdoor grime accumulates. Winter is the time to build SEO infrastructure for spring.

## Website Architecture

### Service Pages

**House Washing Page:** Cover your washing process, the difference between pressure washing and soft washing for siding, materials you wash (vinyl, wood, stucco, brick, stone, Hardie board), cleaning solutions used, preparation steps (closing windows, moving plants), expected results, and pricing ranges per square foot or per story. Address the question every homeowner asks: "Will pressure washing damage my siding?"

**Driveway and Concrete Cleaning Page:** Cover surface types (concrete, pavers, stamped concrete, asphalt), stain removal capabilities (oil, rust, tire marks, algae), sealing options offered after washing, pricing per square foot, and before-and-after photos of restored driveways.

**Roof Cleaning Page:** This page requires extra attention because roof cleaning involves safety and material concerns. Cover soft wash vs. pressure washing for roofs (soft wash is correct for most roof types), types of stains removed (algae, moss, lichen, black streaks from Gloeocapsa magma), materials you clean (shingle, tile, metal), the no-pressure soft wash process, effect on roof lifespan, and pricing. Include strong before-and-after photos.

**Deck and Patio Page:** Cover wood deck restoration (cleaning, brightening, sealing), composite deck cleaning, concrete patio washing, paver cleaning and re-sanding, and the difference between cleaning methods for different deck materials. Pricing by square foot.

**Commercial Pressure Washing Page:** Cover building washing, storefront cleaning, sidewalk and parking area maintenance, grease trap area cleaning for restaurants, fleet washing, and ongoing maintenance contracts. Emphasize scheduling flexibility and your capacity for large commercial properties.

### Area Pages

Pressure washing companies often cover large service areas. Build area pages for every city with content referencing local conditions that drive demand: coastal salt spray, high humidity promoting algae growth, tree pollen accumulation in spring, and specific neighborhood characteristics.
