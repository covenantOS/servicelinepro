# Google Ads for HVAC Companies

HVAC carries the highest cost per click of any home service category in Google Ads. Emergency AC repair keywords in competitive markets hit $80 to $145 per click. Furnace repair keywords during cold snaps reach $60 to $100. Equipment replacement terms average $30 to $65 year-round. These numbers scare most HVAC companies away from Google Ads entirely, or worse, cause them to run timid campaigns with low bids that never appear on the first page.

Here is the reality: HVAC Google Ads at $145 per click can be wildly profitable. A single AC replacement generates $5,000 to $12,000 in revenue. A furnace installation produces $3,000 to $8,000. A maintenance agreement signed through a Google Ads landing page generates $200 to $400 per year in recurring revenue for 5 to 10 years. When your campaign converts 10 percent of clicks into leads and 30 percent of leads into customers, even $100-per-click keywords produce a 5x return on ad spend.

The key is not avoiding expensive keywords. It is building campaigns that extract maximum value from every click. That means precise keyword targeting, high-converting landing pages, smart bid management, and ruthless elimination of wasted spend.

## Campaign Structure for HVAC Companies

HVAC demand splits across emergency repairs, equipment replacement, and maintenance. Each category requires its own campaign with distinct keywords, bids, ad copy, and landing pages.

### Campaign 1: Emergency AC Repair (Summer)

Activate this campaign in late April and run it through September. Budget allocation: 30 to 40 percent of summer spend.

**Keywords:**
- "AC repair near me"
- "Air conditioner not working"
- "AC not blowing cold air"
- "Emergency AC repair"
- "AC stopped working"
- "Central air not cooling"

**Bid strategy:** Target impression share at 90%+ for top-of-page. During heat waves, you cannot afford to lose impression share. A homeowner whose AC dies on a 105-degree day will call the first company they see. If that is not you, they will not scroll to find you.

**Ad copy:** Speed and availability. "AC Broken? We Fix It Today. Licensed HVAC Techs Available Now. Call (555) 123-4567." During extreme heat events, add urgency: "Heat Wave Emergency. Same-Hour AC Repair. Call Now."

### Campaign 2: Emergency Heating Repair (Winter)

Mirror image of the AC campaign for heating season. Activate October through March.

**Keywords:**
- "Furnace repair near me"
- "Heater not working"
- "Furnace blowing cold air"
- "No heat in house"
- "Emergency heating repair"
- "Furnace stopped working"

**Bid strategy:** Same as AC emergency. Target impression share for top positions during cold snaps. Heating emergencies carry the same urgency as cooling emergencies, and the conversion rates are comparable.

### Campaign 3: AC Replacement/Installation

Year-round campaign with seasonal bid adjustments. Targets homeowners who know their AC needs replacement.

**Keywords:**
- "AC replacement cost"
- "New air conditioner installation"
- "Central air installation cost"
- "How much does a new AC cost"
- "AC unit replacement"
- "SEER rating AC"

**Bid strategy:** Target CPA with a ceiling of $200 to $400. AC replacement jobs average $5,000 to $12,000, so a $300 cost per lead is profitable at a 25% close rate, yielding a cost per acquisition of $1,200 on a $7,000+ job.

**Ad copy:** Focus on value and financing. "New AC Installation From $4,500. 0% Financing Available. Free In-Home Estimate. Licensed & Insured." Include manufacturer names if you carry premium brands: "Authorized Trane Dealer. Carrier Expert Installer."

### Campaign 4: Furnace/Heating Replacement

Similar structure to AC replacement but targeting heating equipment.

**Keywords:**
- "Furnace replacement cost"
- "New furnace installation"
- "Heat pump installation cost"
- "Boiler replacement"
- "Furnace vs heat pump"

**Bid strategy:** Target CPA matching the AC replacement campaign. Heating equipment replacements carry similar ticket values.

### Campaign 5: Maintenance and Tune-Ups

Lower CPC campaign targeting homeowners seeking preventive maintenance. These leads have lower immediate value but convert into maintenance agreement customers with high lifetime value.

**Keywords:**
- "AC tune-up near me"
- "HVAC maintenance"
- "Furnace tune-up"
- "Air conditioning service"
- "HVAC inspection"

**Bid strategy:** Maximize conversions with a modest CPA target of $30 to $60. Tune-up appointments cost $80 to $200, and the immediate revenue barely covers acquisition cost. The value comes from converting tune-up customers into maintenance agreement members and positioning your technicians inside the home to identify equipment replacement opportunities.

**Ad copy:** Lead with a specific offer. "$89 AC Tune-Up Special. 22-Point Inspection. All Brands Serviced." A specific price in the ad pre-qualifies clicks and sets expectations.

## Seasonal Bid Management

HVAC bid management requires more active seasonal adjustment than any other home service trade.

### Pre-Season Ramp (2 to 4 weeks before peak)

Increase bids gradually before peak demand hits. Google's algorithms perform better with gradual bid changes than sudden spikes. Start increasing bids in mid-April for cooling season and mid-September for heating season. This builds campaign momentum and quality score before the flood of searches arrives.

### Peak Season (First heat wave / first cold snap)

The first extreme weather event of the season triggers the biggest single-day search volume spike. Increase daily budgets by 100 to 200 percent for the first 3 to 5 days of extreme weather. These days produce the highest conversion rates of the entire year because demand overwhelms supply. Homeowners who cannot get their regular HVAC company on the phone search Google and call whoever appears first.

### Mid-Season Maintenance

After the initial spike, search volume settles into a steady elevated level. Maintain increased bids but optimize based on performance data. Identify which keywords are producing leads at profitable CPAs and which are burning budget. Shift spend from underperforming keywords to top performers.

### Off-Season Strategy

Do not shut off Google Ads during shoulder seasons. Equipment failures happen year-round, and competition drops significantly during mild weather. A keyword that costs $80 per click during a heat wave might cost $25 during a mild October week. The leads are fewer but the cost per lead plummets.

Use off-season campaigns to promote maintenance agreements, indoor air quality services, and equipment upgrades. These services are less time-sensitive than emergency repairs, and the homeowners searching for them during off-peak periods are deliberate planners who convert at high rates.

## Landing Page Optimization for HVAC

With CPCs above $50, every lost conversion is expensive. Your landing pages must convert at the highest possible rate to justify HVAC bid levels.

### Emergency Repair Landing Pages

- Phone number: 72px font, center of the hero, clickable
- Headline: "AC Repair Available Now" or "Furnace Repair. Same-Day Service."
- Three trust signals in a bar below the hero: Licensed, Insured, X Years Experience
- Brief form: Name, Phone, Address, Problem Description (dropdown)
- "We charge by the job, not by the hour" messaging (if applicable)
- Three testimonials from emergency repair customers
- No navigation menu. No footer links. No escape routes.

### Equipment Replacement Landing Pages

- Headline addressing the financial concern: "New AC From $4,500. Monthly Payments as Low as $89."
- Equipment comparison section (show 2 to 3 options at different price points)
- Financing callout with pre-qualification link
- Process timeline: "From estimate to installation in 48 hours"
- Manufacturer certification badges
- Before-and-after photos of installations
- Free estimate form with scheduling capability

### Maintenance Landing Pages

- Specific offer in the headline: "$89 AC Tune-Up. 22-Point Inspection."
- What is included (detailed checklist)
- Maintenance agreement upsell with tier comparison
- Online scheduling widget
- Energy savings calculator or infographic

## Call Tracking and Revenue Attribution

HVAC companies must track leads from click to closed revenue to understand true campaign profitability.

### Dynamic Call Tracking

Use dynamic number insertion to assign unique phone numbers to each keyword and campaign. When a homeowner calls from your AC repair landing page, you know exactly which keyword they searched, which ad they clicked, and which campaign served the ad.

### Call Recording and Scoring

Record every call with legal disclosure. Score calls as:
- **Qualified lead:** Homeowner needs HVAC service, is within your service area, and has intent to hire
- **Unqualified:** Wrong number, solicitor, existing customer calling for non-revenue reasons
- **Appointment set:** Call resulted in a scheduled appointment

This scoring lets you calculate true cost per qualified lead, not just cost per call. An HVAC campaign might generate 100 calls per month, but if 35 are unqualified, your real cost per lead is 35% higher than the raw numbers suggest.

### Offline Conversion Tracking

Feed appointment and revenue data back into Google Ads. When Google's algorithm knows that a $50 click from "AC replacement cost" resulted in a $8,000 job, it optimizes future bidding to find more of those high-value clicks. Without offline conversion data, the algorithm treats a $200 repair lead and a $10,000 replacement lead identically.

## Equipment Replacement Keywords

Equipment replacement keywords deserve special attention because they represent the highest revenue opportunities in HVAC PPC.

### Brand-Specific Keywords

Bid on equipment brand searches:
- "Trane AC installation"
- "Carrier furnace replacement"
- "Lennox heat pump cost"
- "Rheem air conditioner price"
- "Goodman HVAC system"

Homeowners searching brand names are in the active buying phase. They have already decided to replace their equipment and are researching specific products. These leads close at higher rates than generic "AC replacement" searches.

### Comparison Keywords

- "Trane vs Carrier"
- "Heat pump vs furnace cost"
- "Central air vs mini split"
- "Gas furnace vs electric heat pump"

These searches indicate a homeowner deep in the research phase who needs expert guidance. Your ad should position your company as the knowledgeable advisor: "Not Sure Which HVAC System Is Right For You? Free In-Home Consultation. We Install All Major Brands."

### Energy Efficiency Keywords

- "High efficiency furnace"
- "SEER rating explained"
- "Energy efficient AC"
- "ENERGY STAR HVAC"

These searches often come from environmentally conscious or cost-conscious homeowners willing to invest in premium equipment. The job values from these leads trend 20 to 40 percent higher than average.

## Negative Keywords for HVAC

HVAC keywords attract substantial irrelevant traffic without active negative keyword management.

**Immediate additions:**
- DIY, how to, fix myself, tutorial, youtube
- Jobs, career, hiring, salary, technician school, trade school
- Parts, wholesale, supply house, refrigerant, freon (supply buyers)
- Free, cheap (low-quality leads)
- Commercial, industrial (unless you serve commercial)
- Car, auto, vehicle (auto AC searches)
- Window unit, portable (not central HVAC)
- Thermostat (unless you install smart thermostats)

**Ongoing management:** Review search terms reports weekly. HVAC terms trigger ads for unrelated searches constantly. "Air conditioning" shows ads for "air conditioning unit for car." "Heat" shows ads for "heat treatment" and "heat rash." "Furnace" triggers "blast furnace" and "furnace in Minecraft." Aggressive negative keyword management prevents 20 to 30 percent of typical HVAC ad spend from being wasted.

## Retargeting for HVAC Equipment Sales

Equipment replacement decisions involve weeks of research. Retargeting keeps your company visible throughout this process.

Set up display retargeting for visitors to your equipment replacement landing pages. Show them ads featuring:
- The specific equipment type they researched (AC, furnace, heat pump)
- Financing offers
- Customer testimonials about equipment installations
- Seasonal urgency messaging as peak season approaches

Set retargeting windows based on decision timelines:
- Emergency repair visitors: 7 days (they decide immediately or the crisis passes)
- Equipment replacement visitors: 90 days (the research phase is long)
- Maintenance visitors: 30 days (they are ready to schedule but may procrastinate)
