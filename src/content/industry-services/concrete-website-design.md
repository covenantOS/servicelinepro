## Homepage Structure for Concrete Companies

The homepage of a concrete contractor website should function like a visual portfolio with conversion points woven throughout.

### Hero Section

Lead with your most impressive project photo. A freshly stamped and sealed patio in golden afternoon light. A sweeping exposed aggregate driveway. A polished concrete floor reflecting studio lighting. The hero image must be stunning because it sets the quality expectation for the entire visit. Overlay a clear headline: "Concrete Driveways, Patios, and Decorative Work in [City]." Include your phone number and estimate request button.

### Service Cards

Present your service categories in a visual grid. Driveways, patios, stamped concrete, pool decks, sidewalks and steps, foundations, commercial work. Each card features a representative photo, a one-sentence description, and a link to the dedicated service page. This grid acts as a navigation device, letting visitors jump to exactly the service they need.

### Trust Section

Google review count and rating. Years in business. Square footage poured. Number of projects completed. Certifications and memberships. Insurance and bonding status. This data-driven trust section gives homeowners the confidence markers they need before investing thousands of dollars.

### Featured Projects

A curated selection of four to six recent projects with large photos, brief descriptions, and links to the full gallery. Rotate these seasonally: patio and pool deck projects in spring and summer, driveway and garage floor projects in fall, interior polished concrete in winter.

### Process Section

Concrete installation is opaque to most homeowners. They do not know what base preparation involves, how long concrete takes to cure, or when they can drive on their new driveway. A clear process section (Estimate, Design, Preparation, Pour, Finish, Cure) demystifies the experience and reduces the anxiety that prevents homeowners from requesting estimates.

### Testimonials

Three to five reviews from customers who describe the project outcome, the professionalism of your crew, and their satisfaction with the finished product. Reviews that mention specific services ("our stamped patio looks incredible") resonate with visitors researching that same service.

## Service Pages for Concrete Contractors

Each concrete service demands its own dedicated page. These pages do the heavy lifting for both SEO and conversion.

### Driveway Page

Cover removal and replacement of existing driveways, new construction driveways, finish options (broom finish, exposed aggregate, stamped, colored), thickness and reinforcement specifications, base preparation requirements, expansion joint placement, curing timeline, when the homeowner can park on it, maintenance and sealing recommendations, and pricing ranges per square foot. Include a gallery of driveway projects showing different finishes and sizes.

### Stamped Concrete Page

This is your premium service page and should reflect that positioning. Cover available patterns with photos of each (ashlar slate, random stone, wood plank, herringbone, cobblestone, flagstone), color options including integral color, color hardener, and antiquing release combinations, border and accent options, sealer types and resealing schedules, realistic pricing that accounts for the labor intensity of stamped work, and a comparison to alternative materials like pavers and natural stone. The gallery section on this page should be extensive, showing 15 to 20 or more stamped projects in different patterns and colors.

### Patio Page

Backyard patios are emotional purchases tied to outdoor living aspirations. The patio page should address design considerations like size, shape, elevation changes, drainage, integration with landscaping, fire pit and outdoor kitchen pads, seating wall integration, and step-down designs. Include photos of patios in lived-in backyard settings with furniture, plants, and lighting that help homeowners visualize their own space.

### Foundation Page

Foundation work requires a different tone. Homeowners searching for foundation services are often dealing with structural concerns that cause real anxiety. The foundation page should be authoritative and reassuring: types of foundations you pour or repair, signs of foundation problems, your inspection process, repair methods (piering, slabjacking, crack injection), warranty coverage, and structural engineering partnerships. Trust signals are especially important on this page.

### Commercial Page

Commercial decision-makers evaluate contractors differently. Your commercial page should emphasize project capacity, equipment capabilities, scheduling flexibility (weekend and after-hours pours), compliance with ADA requirements, experience with general contractor partnerships, bonding capacity, and relevant commercial project references. Include photos of parking lots, warehouse floors, loading docks, and retail spaces.

## Project Gallery Architecture

The gallery is the most important section of a concrete contractor website after the homepage. Its design and organization directly impact whether visitors convert.

**Organization by Service:** Primary navigation should sort projects by type: driveways, patios, stamped concrete, pool decks, commercial, foundations. Within each category, show projects in reverse chronological order so the newest work appears first.

**High-Quality Photography:** Concrete photography requires good lighting and proper angles. Wide shots show scale and context. Medium shots show pattern detail and color accuracy. Close-ups show surface texture and finish quality. We recommend photographing every completed project before the homeowner moves furniture onto it.

**Project Metadata:** Each gallery entry needs the service type, square footage, finish type, color(s), pattern (if stamped), neighborhood or city, and any special features (borders, inlays, step-downs). This metadata serves dual purposes: it gives visitors context and it provides keyword-rich content for search engines.

**Before-and-After Pairs:** Driveway and patio replacements are perfect before-and-after candidates. A crumbling, stained old driveway next to the same property with a fresh exposed aggregate surface tells a powerful story. Before-and-after comparisons consistently generate the highest engagement on concrete contractor websites.

**Volume:** Target 50 to 100 plus gallery photos across all categories. New photos should be added monthly. A growing gallery signals an active, busy company and provides continuous fresh content for search engines.

## Mobile Design for Concrete Contractors

Homeowners research concrete contractors on their phones while standing in their driveways looking at cracks, sitting on their back porches imagining new patios, or scrolling during lunch breaks. Your site must perform on mobile.

**Gallery Performance:** Image-heavy galleries are the biggest mobile performance challenge for concrete websites. All images must be WebP format, lazy-loaded, properly sized for mobile viewports, and progressively loaded so the page does not jump as images appear. Our Astro sites handle all of this automatically.

**Tap-to-Call and Estimate Forms:** Phone number clickable on mobile. Estimate forms short, with large input fields and appropriate keyboard types. Submit buttons large enough for thumb taps.

**Readable Content:** Service page content formatted for mobile reading. Short paragraphs, descriptive headings, bulleted specifications, and adequate font sizing. No horizontal scrolling on tables or specification lists.
