# Website Design for Painting Companies

A painting company sells a visual transformation. The homeowner's dull, faded exterior becomes crisp and inviting. Their dated oak kitchen cabinets become modern white. Their boring beige living room becomes a sophisticated sage green. Your website must show these transformations with the same visual impact they have in real life.

This makes painting websites fundamentally different from most home service sites. A plumbing website sells reliability and availability. An HVAC website sells comfort and expertise. A painting website sells beauty. The design, the photography, and the visual presentation of your portfolio carry more weight than your copy, your credentials, or your pricing combined.

The second priority is frictionless estimate requests. Painting projects require in-person estimates (or at minimum video/photo estimates), so your website's job is to get the homeowner to request that estimate. Every design decision should reduce the distance between "I want my house painted" and "I just requested an estimate."

## Portfolio Gallery Design

Your portfolio gallery is the centerpiece of your website. It is the page visitors spend the most time on and the page that most directly influences their decision to request an estimate.

### Gallery Architecture

Organize your gallery with filterable categories:
- **Exterior Residential:** Full home repaints, trim and fascia, stucco, brick, siding
- **Interior Residential:** Whole home, individual rooms, hallways and staircases
- **Cabinet Painting:** Kitchen cabinets, bathroom vanities, built-in bookcases
- **Deck and Fence:** Deck staining, fence staining, pergola finishing
- **Commercial:** Offices, retail spaces, apartments, HOA projects

Let visitors filter by category and browse only the project type they are interested in.

### Individual Project Showcases

Each project in the gallery should expand into a full showcase page with:

**Before-and-after photos.** Three to five photo pairs per project. Include wide shots showing the full transformation and detail shots showing paint quality, trim work, and prep quality. Use a slider comparison tool that lets visitors drag between before and after on the same image.

**Project details:**
- Neighborhood/area
- Home style or building type
- Surfaces painted (walls, trim, ceilings, cabinets, exterior)
- Paint brand and color names (e.g., "Benjamin Moore Simply White OC-117 on cabinets, Sherwin-Williams Accessible Beige SW 7036 on walls")
- Prep work performed (scraping, sanding, priming, wood repair)
- Project timeline
- Approximate square footage

**Why this level of detail matters:** Homeowners searching for paint color inspiration find your project showcases through Google. "Benjamin Moore Simply White kitchen cabinets" is a search query that your project showcase page can rank for. Each project page becomes a unique SEO asset with neighborhood keywords, color keywords, and service keywords.

### Photo Quality Standards

Invest in consistent, professional-quality photography for your portfolio. Follow these standards:

- Shoot before and after photos from the same angle
- Use natural lighting when possible (open curtains, shoot during daylight)
- Remove clutter from the frame (no painter's tape visible in "after" photos)
- Include at least one wide shot and one detail shot per project
- For exteriors, shoot from the street perspective (how the homeowner sees it daily)
- For interiors, shoot from the doorway perspective (how you enter the room)

Phone cameras are adequate if you follow these composition guidelines. The difference between amateur and professional-looking painting portfolio photos is usually composition and lighting, not camera quality.

## Color Inspiration Content

Homeowners choose painters and choose paint colors in a connected research process. Providing color inspiration content on your website captures visitors in the research phase and positions you as the expert who can both recommend and apply the perfect colors.

### Popular Color Palettes

Create a page (or section) showcasing curated color palettes organized by:

**By room:** Kitchen palettes, bedroom palettes, living room palettes, bathroom palettes, exterior palettes

**By style:** Modern/contemporary, farmhouse, coastal, traditional, mid-century

**By color family:** Whites and creams, grays, blues, greens, warm neutrals

Each palette entry should show a photo of a real project using those colors, list the specific paint color names and numbers, and describe why the palette works for that application.

### Color Consultation Service

If you offer free color consultation, promote it prominently. "Not sure what color to choose? Our experienced painters provide free in-home color consultations. We bring large color samples, test swatches on your actual walls, and help you find the perfect palette." This service differentiates you from competitors and generates goodwill that converts into bookings.

### Paint Brand Integration

Create content around the paint brands you use:
- "Why We Use Benjamin Moore" (quality, durability, color accuracy)
- "Sherwin-Williams vs Benjamin Moore: A Painter's Perspective"
- "The Best Exterior Paints for [State/Climate]"
- "Cabinet Paint: Why We Recommend Benjamin Moore Advance"

This content ranks for brand-related searches, demonstrates expertise, and helps homeowners understand why material quality matters.

## Estimate CTA Optimization

Every page of your website should make it easy to request an estimate. The CTA is the conversion mechanism that turns gallery browsers into leads.

### CTA Design

**Button text:** "Get Your Free Estimate" performs better than "Contact Us," "Submit," or "Get in Touch." The word "free" removes perceived risk and the word "estimate" tells the visitor exactly what they will receive.

**Button placement:** On every page, both in the main content area and in a sticky element (footer bar on mobile, sidebar on desktop).

**Button color:** A contrasting color that stands out from your page design. If your brand colors are blue and white, make the CTA button orange or green. The button must be the most visually prominent element in its section.

### Estimate Form Design

**Required fields:**
- Name
- Phone number
- Email
- Project type (dropdown: exterior, interior, cabinets, deck/fence, commercial)
- Approximate scope (dropdown: one room, 2-3 rooms, whole house interior, whole house exterior, cabinets only)

**Optional fields:**
- Preferred start date
- Address or zip code
- Brief description or notes
- Photo upload (let homeowners share photos of what they want painted)

The photo upload option is particularly valuable for painting companies. A homeowner who uploads photos of their dated cabinets or peeling exterior gives you information that lets you provide a more accurate initial response, which increases your booking rate.

### Response Time Messaging

"Estimates returned within 24 hours" or "We respond to every estimate request by the next business day." Set and communicate a response time commitment. Homeowners who request estimates from three painting companies hire the first one who responds with a professional, detailed estimate.

## Homepage Design

### Hero Section

**Image:** A dramatic before-and-after slider of your best project. The visual impact of seeing a transformation in progress immediately communicates what you do and the quality of your work.

**Headline:** "[City]'s Trusted Painting Professionals Since [Year]." Or a transformation-focused headline: "Expert Painting That Transforms Your Home."

**CTA:** "Get Your Free Estimate" button prominent and clickable.

**Trust bar:** "Licensed | Insured | 5-Year Warranty | 300+ Five-Star Reviews | Benjamin Moore Certified"

### Service Overview

Six visual cards linking to service pages:
1. Exterior House Painting
2. Interior House Painting
3. Cabinet Painting
4. Deck and Fence Staining
5. Commercial Painting
6. Color Consultation

### Featured Projects

Four to six of your best before-and-after transformations. Each links to the full project showcase. Rotate these seasonally (exterior projects in spring/summer, interior in fall/winter).

### Review Section

Five recent reviews with specific mentions of quality, cleanliness, and professionalism. Include the reviewer's first name and the project type.

## Service Pages

### Exterior Painting Page
- Before-and-after exterior gallery (6 to 8 projects)
- Exterior painting process (power washing, scraping, priming, caulking, painting, cleanup)
- Paint product information (what you use and why)
- Pricing ranges by home size
- Timeline expectations
- Warranty details
- Weather and scheduling considerations
- CTA form

### Interior Painting Page
- Before-and-after interior gallery
- Room-by-room pricing estimates
- Your interior painting process (furniture moving, floor protection, cutting, rolling, cleanup)
- Paint sheen guide (flat, eggshell, satin, semi-gloss, high-gloss and where to use each)
- Color consultation offer
- CTA form

### Cabinet Painting Page
- Before-and-after cabinet gallery (this page deserves 8 to 12 examples minimum)
- Cabinet painting process (degreasing, sanding, priming, spraying, clear coat, reassembly)
- Timeline (typically 5 to 7 business days)
- Pricing range ($2,500 to $5,500 for a typical kitchen)
- Material options (Benjamin Moore Advance, conversion varnish, lacquer)
- Cabinet painting vs new cabinets cost comparison
- CTA form

## Mobile Design

Painting website visitors on mobile are primarily browsing galleries and requesting estimates. Optimize for both:

- Photo galleries that load fast and display well on small screens
- Swipeable before-and-after comparisons
- Tap-to-call and estimate request buttons fixed to the bottom of the screen
- Forms that auto-fill from phone contact info where possible
- Image lazy loading to maintain fast page speeds despite photo-heavy content
