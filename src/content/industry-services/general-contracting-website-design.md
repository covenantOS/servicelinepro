## Why Your Current Website Is Costing You Money

Pull up your website on your phone right now. Time how long it takes to load. If it takes more than three seconds, you have already lost half your visitors. Google tracks this. They know that 53% of mobile visitors abandon a site that takes longer than three seconds to load. For a general contractor, each of those lost visitors could represent a $40,000 kitchen remodel or a $100,000 home addition.

Now look at your Google PageSpeed score. Go to pagespeed.web.dev and enter your URL. Most contractor websites score between 30 and 60 on mobile. That score tells Google your site provides a poor user experience. Google does not want to send its users to poor experiences. Your rankings suffer accordingly.

The keyword "web design for contractors" gets 1,300 searches per month with a keyword difficulty score of only 19. That tells us two things: contractors are actively looking for better websites, and there is an opportunity to rank for that term because competition is relatively low. Contractors know their websites need work. Most just do not know where to start.

## What Makes a Contractor Website Convert

Conversion is the act of turning a website visitor into a phone call, form submission, or email. A website with 1,000 monthly visitors and a 2% conversion rate generates 20 leads per month. The same website redesigned to achieve a 5% conversion rate generates 50 leads. Same traffic. More than double the leads. That is the power of conversion-focused design.

### Above-the-Fold Impact

The first thing a visitor sees on your website determines whether they stay or leave. This area, called "above the fold," must communicate three things within five seconds: what you do, where you do it, and how to contact you.

For general contractors, the hero section should feature a high-quality photo of a completed project, not a stock image, not a generic construction site, but a beautiful finished kitchen or a stunning room addition that shows what you are capable of building. The headline should be direct and specific. "General Contractor Serving [Your City] Since [Year]" works. "Welcome to Our Website" does not.

A phone number and contact form must be visible without scrolling. Homeowners who are ready to call should not have to hunt for your number.

### Portfolio That Sells

Your portfolio is the most important section of your entire website. Homeowners hire general contractors based on the quality of previous work. A strong portfolio does more selling than any amount of clever copywriting.

We build portfolio sections organized by project type: kitchen remodels, bathroom renovations, home additions, whole-house renovations, outdoor living spaces. Each project gets its own page with before-and-after photos, project details (scope, timeline, materials used), and a brief description of the homeowner's goals and how you achieved them.

This structure serves SEO and conversion simultaneously. Individual project pages target long-tail keywords like "modern kitchen remodel" or "craftsman home addition." The detailed project descriptions give homeowners confidence in your ability to handle their specific project type.

### Service Pages That Rank

Each service you offer needs its own dedicated page. This is non-negotiable for SEO and for user experience. A homeowner searching "kitchen remodel contractor" who lands on a generic services page listing 15 different services in bullet points will leave. A homeowner who lands on a detailed kitchen remodeling page with kitchen project photos, kitchen-specific FAQs, and kitchen remodeling pricing information will stay and convert.

We build service pages for every major category in your business:

Kitchen Remodeling. Bathroom Renovation. Home Additions. Whole-House Renovation. Outdoor Living and Decks. Basement Finishing. Structural Repairs. Commercial Tenant Improvements.

Each page includes 1,500 to 2,500 words of original content, project photos from that specific service category, pricing information, process descriptions, FAQ sections with schema markup, and internal links to related services and service areas.

### Trust Signals Everywhere

General contractors face an uphill trust battle online. Homeowners are about to hand someone the keys to their home and write checks totaling tens of thousands of dollars. Every element of your website must reinforce that you are trustworthy, competent, and established.

Trust signals include: your contractor license number displayed prominently, insurance and bonding information, years in business, association memberships (NARI, NAHB, local builders associations), Better Business Bureau rating, customer reviews and testimonials, manufacturer certifications and warranties, and real photos of your team.

We weave these signals throughout every page, not in a single "Why Choose Us" section that visitors may never scroll to, but in headers, sidebars, footers, and inline with service descriptions. A homeowner should encounter trust signals no matter where they are on your site.

### Mobile-First Design

More than 70% of homeowners searching for a general contractor do so on a mobile device. That means your website must be designed for phones first and adapted for desktops second. This is the opposite of how most contractor websites are built.

Mobile-first design means tap-friendly buttons, readable text without pinching and zooming, fast-loading images optimized for cellular connections, click-to-call phone numbers, and forms that are easy to fill out on a small screen. We test every page on multiple device sizes before launch.

## SEO-First Architecture

Every website we build for general contractors is architected for search engine optimization from the first wireframe. This is not an afterthought bolted on after the design is finished. The site structure, URL hierarchy, internal linking, content organization, and technical markup are all planned with Google's ranking factors in mind.

### URL Structure

Clean, descriptive URLs organized in a logical hierarchy. Your kitchen remodeling page lives at /services/kitchen-remodeling/, not /page-id-347/ or /services/?id=kitchen. Google reads URLs and uses them as relevance signals. Human-readable URLs also earn more clicks in search results.

### Internal Linking

Every service page links to related services and service areas. Every area page links to available services. This internal linking structure distributes ranking authority across your entire site and helps Google discover and index every page. It also keeps visitors engaged longer by guiding them to related content.

### Schema Markup

We implement structured data markup on every page. LocalBusiness schema identifies your company details. Service schema describes each service offering. FAQPage schema marks up your FAQ sections for potential rich snippet display in search results. BreadcrumbList schema provides navigational context. This structured data helps Google understand your content and can earn enhanced search result displays that increase click-through rates.

### Page Speed Optimization

Beyond choosing Astro or WordPress, we optimize every image, minify every stylesheet, and eliminate every unnecessary resource that could slow your site down. Images are served in WebP format at appropriate dimensions. Fonts are self-hosted to avoid render-blocking requests to Google Fonts. No unnecessary third-party scripts are loaded.
