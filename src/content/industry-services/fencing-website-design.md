## What a Fence Company Website Must Accomplish

A fence company website operates as a digital showroom. Homeowners visit to evaluate your work quality, material options, pricing range, and professionalism before requesting an estimate. Unlike emergency service businesses where speed drives the decision, fence installation involves research, comparison, and deliberation. Your website needs to support this longer decision process while providing clear paths to conversion at every stage.

The best fence company websites accomplish five things. They showcase completed projects in organized, browsable galleries. They educate homeowners about material options, costs, and trade-offs. They establish trust through reviews, certifications, and business history. They demonstrate local expertise through area-specific content. And they make requesting an estimate effortless from any page on the site.

## Homepage Design for Fence Companies

Your homepage sets the tone for every visit. Fence company homepages need to establish credibility fast while guiding visitors toward the specific material or service they are researching.

### Hero Section

The hero section should feature a striking photo of your best fence installation work. Not a stock photo of a generic white picket fence. A real project from your portfolio that shows craftsmanship, scale, and curb appeal. The headline should be clear and local: "Professional Fence Installation in [City]." Include your phone number and a "Get a Free Estimate" button above the fold.

### Material Selection Grid

Below the hero, present your material options in a clean grid. Vinyl, wood, aluminum, chain link, wrought iron, composite. Each card displays a representative photo, a brief description, a starting price per linear foot, and a link to the dedicated material page. This grid lets homeowners self-select into their area of interest immediately, driving them deeper into your site.

### Trust Bar

Display your Google review count and rating, years in business, number of fences installed, and key certifications in a horizontal trust bar. This section answers the silent question every visitor asks: "Can I trust this company with a $5,000 to $15,000 project?"

### Featured Projects Carousel

Show four to six of your most impressive recent installations with large photos, brief descriptions (material, style, linear footage, neighborhood), and links to the full gallery. This carousel gives visitors a taste of your portfolio quality without requiring them to navigate away from the homepage.

### Service Area Map

A map highlighting your coverage area helps visitors confirm you serve their location. For fence companies covering a metro area with multiple cities and suburbs, this visual confirmation reduces out-of-area inquiries.

### How It Works Section

Walk the homeowner through your process in three to five steps: request an estimate, on-site consultation, material selection, permit handling, and installation. Fence installation feels like a big, complicated project to most homeowners. Showing them a clear, simple process reduces anxiety and moves them closer to requesting that estimate.

### Review Section

Display three to five strong customer reviews with full names, star ratings, and the type of fence installed. Reviews that mention specific materials, the installation experience, and the final result resonate most with prospective customers.

## Material Pages That Rank and Convert

Each fencing material you offer needs its own dedicated page. These pages serve dual duty: they capture organic search traffic for material-specific keywords and they provide the detailed information homeowners need to make purchasing decisions.

### Vinyl Fence Page

Vinyl fencing is the fastest-growing segment in residential fencing. Your vinyl page should cover: brands you install and why you chose them, color and texture options (woodgrain, stone, smooth), height options from 4-foot picket to 8-foot privacy, gate styles and hardware, wind load ratings, UV resistance, warranty terms (most premium vinyl comes with lifetime warranties), maintenance requirements (near zero), and pricing per linear foot installed. Include a gallery of vinyl installations showing different styles, colors, and applications.

### Wood Fence Page

Wood fencing remains the most popular material in many markets. Cover: wood species available (cedar, pine, redwood, cypress), treatment options (pressure-treated, stained, sealed), style options (dog-ear, board-on-board, shadowbox, cap-and-trim, horizontal slat), post options (wood posts vs. steel posts in concrete), expected lifespan by species, maintenance schedule, and pricing. Address the common concern about wood fence longevity compared to vinyl, and explain how proper installation and maintenance extend wood fence life to 15 to 25 years.

### Aluminum Fence Page

Ornamental aluminum fencing appeals to homeowners who want the look of wrought iron without the maintenance. Cover: style grades (residential, commercial, industrial), color options (typically black, bronze, white), height options, rake-ability for sloped terrain, pool code compliance, puppy panel add-ons, gate automation compatibility, and pricing. Aluminum fence pages should emphasize aesthetics and property value enhancement.

### Chain Link Fence Page

Chain link is the most affordable fencing option and dominates commercial, industrial, and budget-conscious residential applications. Cover: gauge options (heavier gauge = more durable), coating options (galvanized, vinyl-coated in green or black), height options, privacy slat additions, barbed wire or razor wire options for commercial, gate options including sliding gates for driveways, and pricing. Do not treat chain link as an afterthought. It is a high-volume product with significant revenue potential.

## Project Gallery Design

The project gallery is one of the most-visited sections of a fence company website. Its design directly impacts whether visitors request estimates.

**Organization:** Sort projects by material type first, then by style within each material. A homeowner researching vinyl privacy fencing should be able to filter or navigate directly to vinyl privacy fence photos without scrolling through wood picket and chain link projects.

**Photo Quality:** Use high-resolution photos taken in good lighting. Show the full fence installation from multiple angles: a wide shot showing the property context, a medium shot showing craftsmanship details like post caps and gate hardware, and a close-up showing material texture and finish quality.

**Project Details:** Each gallery entry should include the material, style, height, approximate linear footage, and neighborhood or city. "6-foot Bufftech Certagrain Vinyl Privacy Fence, 280 linear feet, Westchase, FL" gives the visitor specific information they can relate to their own project.

**Before-and-After Pairs:** Where possible, show the property before the fence and after. The transformation from an open, undefined lot to a private, enclosed backyard is visually powerful and emotionally compelling.

**Volume:** Aim for 50 to 100 or more gallery photos across all materials and styles. A large gallery demonstrates experience and capability. A sparse gallery with 8 photos raises questions about your track record.

## Area Pages for Fence Companies

Fence companies that serve multiple cities need dedicated area pages for each municipality. These pages capture "[fence service] in [city]" keywords and demonstrate local knowledge.

Effective fence company area pages include:

**Local Building Code Information:** Fence permit requirements vary by city. Some municipalities require permits for all fences over 4 feet. Others allow 6-foot privacy fences without permits but require them for front-yard installations. Some HOA communities have additional restrictions on materials, colors, and heights. Your area page should reference these specific requirements for each city you serve.

**Property Line and Survey Information:** Fence installations require awareness of property boundaries. Some municipalities require a survey before fence installation. Others have specific setback requirements. Including this information demonstrates expertise and helps the homeowner prepare for their project.

**Neighborhood Character:** Different neighborhoods within a metro have different fencing needs. Older established neighborhoods may have existing fence aesthetics that new installations should complement. Newer subdivisions often have HOA-mandated materials and styles. Rural-adjacent suburbs may need ranch or agricultural fencing. Waterfront properties have wind load considerations.

**Completed Projects:** Reference specific projects completed in that area. "We recently installed 350 feet of 6-foot cedar privacy fencing in [neighborhood]" creates local relevance and social proof.

## Mobile Experience for Fence Websites

Fence research often begins on mobile. A homeowner standing in their yard, looking at a neighbor's fence or measuring their property line, pulls out their phone and searches for fence contractors. Your site must perform on that device.

Key mobile design elements for fence companies:

**Fast image loading.** Fence galleries are image-heavy. All photos must be served in WebP format, properly sized for the viewport, and lazy-loaded below the fold. Our Astro sites handle this automatically.

**Easy gallery browsing.** Swiping through project photos on mobile must be smooth and responsive. Images should fill the screen width and load instantly as the visitor swipes.

**Tap-to-call.** The phone number in the header must be clickable on mobile. Estimate request CTAs should be prominent and accessible without extensive scrolling.

**Readable specifications.** Material specifications, pricing ranges, and warranty details must be formatted for mobile reading. Tables should be responsive. Bulleted lists should replace dense paragraphs where appropriate.

**Quick estimate form.** The mobile estimate form should have minimal fields, appropriate keyboard types, and large tap targets. Auto-fill should work for name, email, and phone fields.

## SEO Architecture Built In

Every fence company website we build includes foundational SEO elements:

**Unique Title Tags:** Each page has a keyword-optimized title tag. "Vinyl Fence Installation in [City] | [Company Name]" for material pages. "[City] Fence Company | Installation, Repair, Gates | [Company Name]" for area pages.

**Schema Markup:** LocalBusiness schema sitewide, Service schema on material and service pages, FAQPage schema on FAQ sections, ImageGallery schema on the project gallery, and BreadcrumbList schema for navigation.

**Internal Linking:** Material pages link to the gallery, the gallery links back to material pages, area pages link to all available services, and every page links to the estimate request form. This linking structure distributes authority and keeps visitors engaged.

**Image Alt Text:** Every gallery photo includes descriptive alt text: "6-foot vinyl privacy fence installation in [neighborhood], [city]." This supports image search traffic and accessibility.

**Blog Integration:** A blog section allows ongoing content publication targeting informational keywords like "best fence material for dogs" and "how much does fence installation cost." Blog posts link to service pages, building topical authority.
