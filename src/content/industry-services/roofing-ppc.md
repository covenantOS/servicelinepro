# Google Ads for Roofing Companies

Roofing is one of the most profitable industries for Google Ads, and one of the most dangerous. A well-managed campaign generates leads for $50 to $150 that convert into $5,000 to $15,000 jobs. A poorly managed campaign burns through $10,000 a month with nothing to show for it. The difference comes down to understanding how roofing customers search, when they search, and what makes them pick up the phone.

Google Ads for roofing companies requires a fundamentally different approach than most home service trades. Roofing demand is driven by weather events, seasonal cycles, and insurance claims. A plumber gets steady emergency calls year-round. A roofer might get 60% of their annual leads in a three-month window after a major storm. Your PPC strategy needs to account for this volatility.

## Campaign Structure for Roofing Companies

The biggest mistake roofing companies make with Google Ads is running a single campaign targeting every roofing keyword. Different types of roofing searches require different campaigns with different budgets, bids, and ad copy.

### Campaign 1: Emergency and Storm Damage

This campaign targets homeowners with active roof damage who need immediate help. These searches carry the highest purchase intent in all of roofing PPC.

**Target keywords:**
- "Emergency roof repair near me"
- "Roof leak repair"
- "Storm damage roof repair"
- "Hail damage roofer"
- "Roof tarping service"
- "Wind damage roof repair"

**Bid strategy:** Maximize conversions with a target CPA based on your average emergency repair ticket value. For a $1,500 average repair ticket, a $100 CPA is highly profitable. Bid aggressively on these terms because the conversion rate is 15 to 25 percent and the lead quality is exceptional.

**Ad copy focus:** Speed and availability. "Emergency Roof Repair. Available Now. Call For Same-Day Service." Homeowners with active leaks do not compare prices. They call the first roofer who sounds like they can show up today.

**Schedule:** Run 24/7 during storm seasons. Reduce to business hours during calm weather periods but never turn off completely. Roof leaks happen year-round.

### Campaign 2: Roof Replacement

This campaign targets homeowners who know they need a new roof and are shopping for contractors. These leads have the highest ticket value but longer decision timelines.

**Target keywords:**
- "Roof replacement cost [city]"
- "New roof installation"
- "Roofing company near me"
- "Best roofer in [city]"
- "Roof replacement estimate"
- "Shingle roof replacement"
- "Metal roof installation cost"

**Bid strategy:** Target impression share for top-of-page positions. Roof replacement searches have high CPCs ($15 to $45) but the job values ($8,000 to $20,000) justify aggressive bidding. Use a target CPA of $150 to $250 for replacement leads.

**Ad copy focus:** Trust signals, financing, and material expertise. "Licensed & Insured Roofing Contractor. GAF Master Elite Certified. Free Estimates. Financing Available." Homeowners replacing a roof want confidence in the contractor's quality and credentials.

**Schedule:** Heavy during spring and summer. Reduce in winter for cold-climate markets. In warm-climate markets like Florida, Texas, and Arizona, run year-round with seasonal bid adjustments.

### Campaign 3: Insurance Restoration

This campaign targets homeowners navigating the insurance claim process for roof damage. These are the highest-value leads in residential roofing.

**Target keywords:**
- "Roof insurance claim help"
- "Does insurance cover roof replacement"
- "Roofer that works with insurance"
- "Insurance roof inspection"
- "Supplemental roof claim"
- "Roof damage claim denied"

**Bid strategy:** Manual CPC with high bids. Insurance restoration leads convert into full roof replacements paid by insurance companies. The average ticket is $10,000 to $15,000. Paying $75 per click with a 10% conversion rate means a $750 cost per lead for a job worth $10,000+. That math works all day.

**Ad copy focus:** Insurance expertise and homeowner advocacy. "We Handle Your Insurance Claim From Start to Finish. Free Inspection. No Out-of-Pocket Costs Beyond Your Deductible." Homeowners dealing with insurance companies feel overwhelmed. Position your company as the expert who takes that burden off their plate.

**Schedule:** Activate within 48 hours of major storm events. Run continuously in storm-prone markets. In markets with infrequent storms, keep the campaign paused and activate when weather events create demand.

### Campaign 4: Call-Only

Call-only campaigns display your phone number directly in the ad. Tapping the ad initiates a phone call instead of loading a landing page. For roofing, call-only campaigns produce the highest conversion rates of any campaign type.

**Target keywords:** Mirror your emergency and storm damage campaigns with the highest-intent keywords only. "Roof repair near me," "emergency roofer," "storm damage roof repair."

**Bid strategy:** Set bids 20 to 30 percent higher than your standard search campaigns. Every interaction with a call-only ad is a phone call, so your conversion rate from click to lead approaches 80 to 90 percent (versus 10 to 20 percent for standard campaigns where clicks go to a landing page).

**Schedule:** Business hours only, or whenever your office can answer the phone within 3 rings. Missed calls from call-only campaigns are wasted money with no way to follow up.

## Storm Chasing PPC Strategy

Storm-driven PPC is where roofing companies make or lose their year. A single hailstorm can generate 10x normal search volume for roofing keywords overnight. The companies that capture this surge reap enormous profits. The companies that react too slowly watch competitors fill their schedules.

### Pre-Storm Preparation

Build your storm damage campaigns in advance. Write the ad copy. Set up the landing pages. Configure the targeting. Save the campaigns in paused status. When a storm hits, you need to activate within hours, not build from scratch.

Create storm-specific landing pages that you can update with the actual storm date and location details. A page titled "Hail Damage Roof Repair After the [Date] [City] Storm" with relevant content and photos from similar past work converts dramatically better than a generic storm damage page.

Set up weather alerts for your service area using a commercial weather service. When a significant storm is forecasted, prepare to increase budgets and activate storm campaigns before the storm hits. The first surge of searches happens within hours of the event.

### During Active Storm Events

Increase your budget immediately. If your normal daily budget is $200, increase to $500 to $1,000 during active storm response periods. Search volume spikes 5 to 15 times normal levels, and the first roofers to appear capture the most leads.

Switch emergency and storm damage campaigns to Target Impression Share bidding with a goal of 90%+ top-of-page impression share. During storm events, being visible matters more than cost efficiency. A $100 click that converts into a $12,000 insurance restoration job is still wildly profitable.

Update your ad copy to reference the specific storm. "Free Roof Inspections After Tuesday's Hailstorm. Call Now." Relevance drives click-through rates, and click-through rates affect your Quality Score, which reduces your cost per click.

### Post-Storm Follow-Through

Storm-driven search volume decays over 30 to 60 days after the event. Gradually reduce budgets as volume normalizes. Shift messaging from emergency response to insurance claim assistance as the initial repair surge transitions into full replacement projects.

Retarget website visitors from storm damage landing pages with display ads. Many homeowners visit your page during the initial panic but do not call immediately. Retargeting keeps your company visible during their decision process, which can extend weeks as they navigate insurance claims.

## Seasonal Bid Adjustments

Roofing demand follows predictable seasonal patterns that your bid strategy should mirror.

**Spring (March to May):** Increase bids 15 to 25 percent. This is peak demand for inspections and replacements. Homeowners who delayed fall repairs and those who discovered winter damage all enter the market simultaneously.

**Summer (June to August):** Maintain peak bids for replacement campaigns. Reduce emergency campaign bids slightly unless active storms drive demand. Competition is highest during summer, so monitor CPC trends weekly.

**Fall (September to November):** Keep replacement bids strong through October. Homeowners want projects completed before winter. November bids can decrease 10 to 15 percent as decision-making slows.

**Winter (December to February):** Reduce bids 20 to 40 percent in cold-climate markets where roofing installations stop. In warm-climate markets, winter is still viable for replacements. Redirect winter budget toward brand awareness campaigns and retargeting to fill the spring pipeline.

## Landing Page Strategy for Roofing PPC

Never send Google Ads traffic to your homepage. Build dedicated landing pages for each campaign type.

### Emergency Repair Landing Page

- Phone number in the hero section, large and clickable
- "Available Now" or "24/7 Emergency Service" above the fold
- 3 trust signals: licensed, insured, years of experience
- Short form with name, phone, address, and damage description
- No navigation menu (remove escape routes)
- Customer testimonials focused on speed of response

### Roof Replacement Landing Page

- Before-and-after photo gallery above the fold
- Material options with brief descriptions (asphalt shingles, metal, tile)
- Financing callout with monthly payment example
- Free estimate form with fields for roof size, material preference, and timeline
- Reviews section with star ratings
- Manufacturer certification badges (GAF, Owens Corning, CertainTeed)

### Insurance Restoration Landing Page

- Headline addressing the homeowner's pain: "Storm Damaged Your Roof? We Handle Everything."
- Step-by-step insurance claim process graphic
- "We Work Directly With Your Insurance Company" messaging
- Free inspection offer with scheduling form
- FAQ section addressing common insurance questions
- Photos of previous insurance restoration projects

## Call Tracking and Attribution

Roofing companies generate most of their leads through phone calls. Without call tracking, you cannot measure which keywords, campaigns, and ads produce revenue.

Use dynamic number insertion on your landing pages. Each visitor sees a unique tracking number that ties their call back to the specific keyword they searched, the ad they clicked, and the campaign that served the ad. This data tells you exactly which keywords generate $12,000 roof replacement leads and which generate $200 repair calls.

Record every call (with proper legal disclosure). Listen to a sample of calls weekly to evaluate lead quality. A campaign might generate 50 calls per month, but if 30 of those are homeowners asking about gutter cleaning when they clicked a roof replacement ad, your keyword targeting needs adjustment.

Track calls through to closed revenue. The most valuable metric in roofing PPC is cost per closed job, not cost per click or cost per call. A keyword that costs $45 per click but converts 1 in 5 calls into a $10,000 job has a cost per acquisition of $2,250. That is a 4:1 return on ad spend. A keyword that costs $15 per click but produces low-quality calls that never close is worthless regardless of the low CPC.

## Negative Keyword Management

Roofing PPC requires aggressive negative keyword management to prevent wasted spend.

**Add these negative keywords immediately:**
- DIY, how to, tutorial (searchers who want to fix it themselves)
- Jobs, career, hiring, salary (job seekers)
- Free (typically low-quality leads)
- Wholesale, supplier, materials (people buying materials, not services)
- Commercial (unless you do commercial roofing)
- Specific competitor names (unless you want to bid on competitor terms)

**Review your search terms report weekly.** Google's broad match and phrase match algorithms will show your ads for searches you never intended to target. A "roof repair" keyword might trigger ads for "car roof repair," "roof of mouth pain," or "rooftop restaurant." Add irrelevant search terms as negative keywords the moment you see them.

During storm events, search query variety explodes. Homeowners search in unpredictable ways. Monitor search terms daily during storm response periods to catch and block irrelevant traffic before it drains your elevated budget.

## Competitor Analysis and Positioning

Research your competitors' Google Ads before launching your own. Use Google's Ad Transparency Center or simply search your target keywords and document what competitors are advertising.

Identify gaps in their messaging. If every competitor emphasizes "free estimates," that becomes table stakes rather than a differentiator. Look for what they are not saying. If no competitor mentions financing, lead with financing. If no one highlights manufacturer certifications, make that your headline.

Monitor competitor ad schedules. Some roofing companies only run ads during business hours. If you extend your schedule to evenings and weekends, you capture searches with zero competition. Saturday morning searches from homeowners who just noticed a leak while cleaning gutters are some of the highest-converting queries in roofing.

## Budget Allocation Framework

For a roofing company spending $5,000 per month on Google Ads during normal periods:

- Emergency/Storm Damage Campaign: $1,500 (30%)
- Roof Replacement Campaign: $2,000 (40%)
- Insurance Restoration Campaign: $1,000 (20%)
- Call-Only Campaign: $500 (10%)

During active storm events, redistribute to:

- Emergency/Storm Damage Campaign: $3,000 (40%)
- Insurance Restoration Campaign: $2,500 (33%)
- Roof Replacement Campaign: $1,500 (20%)
- Call-Only Campaign: $500 (7%)

Scale the total budget proportionally to demand. If storm-driven search volume triples, triple your budget for 30 to 60 days to capture the surge. The ROI during storm events is typically 5 to 10x higher than normal periods, so increased spending produces outsized returns.

## Retargeting for Roofing Companies

Roofing decisions, especially full replacements, involve a research phase of days to weeks. Retargeting keeps your company visible throughout this decision process.

Set up Google Display Network retargeting for website visitors who did not convert. Show them ads featuring your strongest trust signals: review count, years in business, manufacturer certifications, and financing options.

Create separate retargeting audiences for each landing page. Someone who visited your storm damage page gets retargeted with storm-specific messaging. Someone who visited your roof replacement page gets retargeted with replacement-focused ads including pricing and financing.

Set retargeting windows based on the typical decision timeline:
- Emergency repair visitors: 7-day window (they decide fast)
- Storm damage visitors: 30-day window (insurance process takes time)
- Roof replacement visitors: 60-day window (longer research phase)

Exclude converters from retargeting immediately. Once someone calls or submits a form, stop showing them ads. This prevents wasting retargeting budget on leads you already captured.
