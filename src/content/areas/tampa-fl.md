## The Tampa Home Service Landscape

Understanding the Tampa market requires understanding the types of homes, the climate demands, and the seasonal patterns that drive service calls.

### Climate-Driven Demand

Tampa's subtropical climate means HVAC systems run almost year-round. Summer temperatures regularly exceed 90 degrees with humidity that makes it feel like 105. AC units work harder and fail more frequently here than in most other markets. This creates constant, predictable demand for HVAC services.

The rainy season from June through September brings daily thunderstorms and occasional severe weather. Roofing companies stay busy with leak repairs, missing shingles, and storm damage claims. Plumbers deal with flooding, drainage issues, and water intrusion. Electricians handle surge damage and panel replacements after lightning strikes.

Hurricane season adds another layer. Even when a major storm does not make direct landfall, tropical weather drives demand for emergency services, preventive maintenance, and post-storm inspections.

### Housing Stock Diversity

Tampa's housing stock spans more than a century. South Tampa and Hyde Park feature historic bungalows and Mediterranean Revival homes from the early 1900s. Seminole Heights has a mix of Craftsman-style homes from the 1920s and 1930s alongside newer infill construction. West Tampa and Ybor City have homes with unique architectural character that require specialized service knowledge.

Move north and east, and the housing gets newer. Carrollwood, built primarily in the 1970s and 1980s, has homes reaching the age where major systems need replacement. New Tampa, developed mostly after 1990, has younger housing stock but still generates significant service demand as those homes age into their 20s and 30s.

The newer developments in Tampa Palms, Hunter's Green, and along the Bruce B. Downs corridor represent the most recent generation of housing. These homes have modern systems but still need regular maintenance and occasional repair.
