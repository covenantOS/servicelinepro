# Marketing for General Contractors

General contracting is the highest-ticket segment of home services. A kitchen remodel runs $30,000 to $80,000. A full home [renovation](/industries/remodeling/) hits $100,000 to $300,000. New construction starts at $200,000 and scales into the millions. These are not impulse purchases. The homeowner who hires a general contractor is making one of the largest financial decisions of their life, and they will scrutinize every detail of your reputation, your portfolio, and your online presence before they make that commitment.

ServiceLine Pro builds marketing systems for general contractors that generate qualified leads for direct-to-consumer renovation and construction projects. We build the online presence that converts skeptical homeowners into confident clients: a project portfolio that proves your capability, a website that communicates professionalism at every level, and search visibility that puts you in front of homeowners when they start researching their next major project.

## The GC Revenue Landscape

General contractors operate across a spectrum of work types, each with different marketing requirements, profit margins, and client acquisition strategies.

### Direct-to-Consumer Renovations

This is the bread and butter of most residential general contractors. Kitchen remodels, bathroom renovations, home additions, basement finishing, and whole-house renovations. Average project values range from $30,000 for a bathroom remodel to $150,000 or more for a major addition or whole-house renovation. The homeowner finds you, gets a proposal, and hires you directly. No middleman.

Direct-to-consumer renovation work is the most marketable category for a GC. The homeowner searches Google, visits your website, reviews your portfolio, reads your reviews, and calls you. Every step of that process can be optimized through marketing. This is where SEO, Google Ads, and a strong website deliver the highest return.

### New Construction

Custom home building is a different business entirely. The average custom home costs $300,000 to $1,000,000 or more depending on the market, and the sales cycle can stretch 6 to 18 months from initial conversation to contract signing. These projects come through a combination of referrals, realtor relationships, lot referrals, and organic search.

Marketing for new construction is less about lead volume and more about positioning. Your website must project a level of sophistication that matches the investment your clients are making. Professional photography, 3D renderings, virtual tours of completed homes, and detailed [case studies](/case-studies/) that walk the prospective buyer through the design and construction process establish the credibility necessary to win $500K+ projects.

### Bid and Spec Work

Commercial projects, public works, and subcontractor relationships follow a bid process that traditional digital marketing cannot influence directly. You do not win a school renovation project through Google Ads. You win it through relationships, pre-qualification, competitive pricing, and a track record of delivering on schedule and within budget.

But marketing supports bid work indirectly. A professional website with a commercial project portfolio, safety certifications, bonding information, and a list of completed public and private projects gives the project owner or general contractor confidence when reviewing your bid. Your online presence serves as a credibility check, and an impressive one tips close decisions in your favor.

## Why Referral-Only Marketing is a Trap

Most general contractors built their businesses on referrals. A homeowner loves their kitchen remodel, tells three friends, and those friends call. The GC stays busy without spending a dollar on advertising. This works until it does not.

Referral-dependent businesses are vulnerable. A slow quarter in referrals means a slow quarter in revenue. A key referral source (a realtor, architect, or past client) moves away or stops referring. The economy shifts and renovation budgets tighten. Suddenly the phone is quiet, and there is no marketing infrastructure in place to fill the gap.

The GCs that build lasting, scalable businesses combine strong referral relationships with predictable digital marketing channels. [SEO](/services/local-seo/) generates a baseline of organic leads every month. [Google Ads](/services/ppc/) can be scaled up during slow periods and dialed back when the schedule is full. The [website](/services/website-design/) works around the clock, converting visitors into inquiries whether you are on the job site or asleep.

This does not mean abandoning referrals. It means building a second engine that runs in parallel. When referrals are strong and the digital leads are flowing, you have the luxury of choosing the best projects and commanding premium pricing. When referrals slow down, the digital pipeline prevents the feast-or-famine cycle that kills so many contracting businesses.

## Local SEO for General Contractors

"General contractor near me" generates over 246,000 annual searches nationally. That is a massive pool of homeowners looking for exactly what you offer. The GCs ranking in the Google Map Pack and the top organic positions for this term in their market receive a steady stream of high-value inquiries.

### Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile is the front door for most prospective clients. GCs should select "General Contractor" as their primary category with secondary categories including "Home Builder," "Remodeling Contractor," "Kitchen Remodeler," "Bathroom Remodeler," and any other relevant specialties.

The GBP description should lead with your license number, years in business, and the types of projects you specialize in. Photos should showcase completed projects with professional quality. Do not upload blurry jobsite snapshots. Upload finished kitchens, completed additions, and polished interiors that show what the homeowner will actually get.

Posts on your profile should feature recent project completions with photos and brief descriptions. A post showing a completed kitchen remodel with "Just completed this kitchen renovation in [neighborhood]. Custom cabinetry, quartz countertops, and a 9-foot waterfall island" gives prospective clients a concrete picture of your capabilities.

Review management is non-negotiable. GC projects are high-touch, multi-week or multi-month engagements. You have abundant opportunity to earn reviews from satisfied clients. Ask at project completion when satisfaction is highest. A GC with 100+ reviews and a 4.8-star rating has a competitive moat that takes years for competitors to replicate.

### Project-Specific Service Pages

A general contractor's website needs dedicated pages for each major project type, not a single page that lists everything. Separate pages for kitchen remodels, bathroom renovations, home additions, basement finishing, new construction, and whole-house renovations allow you to target specific search queries, provide relevant information and photos for each project type, and rank for multiple keywords simultaneously.

Each service page should include:

- Detailed description of your process for that project type
- Pricing ranges (homeowners search for "kitchen remodel cost" and "bathroom renovation cost" in enormous volume)
- Photos of completed projects of that type
- Timeline expectations
- Permit and code information
- An FAQ section addressing common questions specific to that project type

A kitchen remodel page that ranks for "kitchen remodel contractor [city]," "kitchen remodel cost," and "how long does a kitchen remodel take" can generate dozens of qualified leads per month from a single page.

### Content That Demonstrates Expertise

General contractors have a unique content advantage: project complexity. A blog post walking through the structural considerations of removing a load-bearing wall, the permitting process for a home addition, or the decision framework for a full gut renovation vs. a cosmetic update demonstrates expertise that separates you from handymen and unlicensed operators.

We build content strategies for GCs around the questions homeowners ask during the planning phase:

"How much does a kitchen remodel cost in [city]?" Write a 2,000-word guide with actual pricing breakdowns by scope level (cosmetic refresh at $15,000 to $25,000, mid-range renovation at $30,000 to $60,000, high-end custom at $60,000 to $120,000).

"Do I need a permit for a bathroom remodel?" Write a guide explaining when permits are required (moving [plumbing](/industries/plumbing/), [electrical](/industries/electrical/) work, structural changes) and when they are not (cosmetic updates, fixture swaps).

"How long does a home addition take?" Walk through the typical timeline from design through permitting through construction, with honest assessment of what causes delays.

This content attracts homeowners in the planning phase, establishes your authority, and positions your company in their consideration set before they ever request a proposal.

## Google Ads for General Contractors

Google Ads for general contractors targets homeowners who have moved past the research phase and are ready to hire. These are high-intent searches with strong conversion potential and significant revenue behind each lead.

### Campaign Architecture

We structure GC Google Ads campaigns around specific project types:

**Kitchen remodel campaigns** target "kitchen remodel contractor near me," "kitchen renovation [city]," "kitchen remodel cost [city]," and "custom kitchen contractor." Kitchen remodels are the most searched renovation category and generate the highest volume of leads.

**Bathroom remodel campaigns** target "bathroom remodel contractor near me," "bathroom renovation [city]," and "master bath remodel cost." Bathroom projects have shorter sales cycles than kitchens and often serve as a gateway to larger renovation contracts.

**Home addition campaigns** target "home addition contractor near me," "room addition cost," "second story addition [city]," and "in-law suite contractor." These are high-ticket searches with average project values of $80,000 to $250,000.

**General renovation campaigns** target "home renovation contractor near me," "whole house remodel [city]," and "general contractor near me." These are broad searches that capture homeowners with various project types.

**New construction campaigns** (where applicable) target "custom home builder [city]," "new home construction cost," and "build a house [city]." These campaigns drive traffic to portfolio-heavy landing pages.

### Landing Page Requirements

GC landing pages must communicate trust immediately. The homeowner is considering a $50,000 to $500,000 investment. Your landing page must include:

- Your general contractor license number and state licensing board link
- Years in business and total projects completed
- Professional project photos (not stock images)
- Customer testimonials with full names and project details
- Insurance and bonding information
- A clear call to action for scheduling a consultation

We test landing pages continuously, optimizing headlines, photo selections, form placements, and social proof elements to maximize conversion rates. A 1 percent improvement in conversion rate on a campaign generating 1,000 visitors per month adds 10 leads, which at GC ticket sizes could represent $500,000 or more in pipeline.

### Budget and ROI

General contractors should invest $4,000 to $10,000 per month in Google Ads depending on market competitiveness and growth goals. The cost per lead for GC services runs $60 to $200, which sounds expensive until you consider the average project value.

A GC spending $6,000 per month and generating 40 leads at $150 each needs to close only 4 projects at an average of $75,000 to produce $300,000 in revenue from a $6,000 investment. That is a 50:1 return. Even accounting for close rates of 15 to 25 percent on Google Ads leads, the math is overwhelmingly positive.

## Website Design for General Contractors

A general contractor's website is a digital showroom. The homeowner who visits your site is looking for proof that you can handle their project with competence, professionalism, and integrity. Every element of your website must reinforce that message.

### The Project Portfolio

This is the centerpiece. A GC without a portfolio is a GC without a closing tool. Professional photography of completed projects, organized by project type and scope, gives homeowners the confidence to request a consultation.

Each portfolio entry should include:

- Multiple professional photos showing key areas of the completed project
- Project scope description (what was done, from demolition to finishing)
- Approximate project value range
- Timeline from start to completion
- Specific materials and features (brand names, custom elements)
- The city or neighborhood where the project was completed
- Any challenges that were overcome during the project

We build portfolio pages with filtering functionality that lets visitors browse by project type, budget range, and location. This makes the portfolio useful for homeowners with specific project types in mind and for Google, which indexes the content on these pages for relevant search queries.

### Licensing as a Trust Signal

In most states, general contractors must hold a state-issued license. This license is a powerful trust signal that most GCs bury in the fine print of their website footer. It should be prominent. Display it on the homepage, on every service page, and in the header or banner area where every visitor sees it.

A link to the state licensing board verification page adds another layer of credibility. The homeowner can click through and verify your license status independently. This transparency builds trust with the homeowner and signals to Google that your site is connected to authoritative, verifiable information.

If you hold additional certifications (NAHB Certified Graduate Builder, NARI Certified Remodeler, EPA Lead-Safe Certified, OSHA certifications, or Energy Star partner status), display them with their logos. These certifications separate you from the unlicensed and uninsured operators who compete on price alone and disappear when problems arise.

### Process Pages That Set Expectations

Major renovations are stressful for homeowners. The construction process is disruptive, expensive, and unfamiliar. A GC website that explains the process, step by step, from initial consultation through design, permitting, construction, and final walkthrough, reduces the homeowner's anxiety and positions your company as organized and professional.

We build detailed process pages that include typical timelines, what the homeowner should expect during each phase, how communication works during the project (weekly updates, project management apps, regular walk-throughs), and how change orders are handled. This level of transparency is rare among GC websites and makes an immediate impression on homeowners who are comparing multiple contractors.

### Financing Information

Many homeowners fund renovations through home equity loans, HELOCs, construction loans, or personal savings. A GC website that addresses financing options and connects homeowners with lending partners removes a barrier to commitment. A page explaining typical financing paths for different project sizes ($30,000 cosmetic renovation vs. $150,000 major renovation vs. $400,000 new construction) helps homeowners understand that their project is financially achievable.
